# Perseverance and God's Righteous Judgment

Second Thessalonians addresses confusion about Christ's return and encourages believers facing persecution.

The day of the Lord is coming, but it has not yet arrived. Persevere in faith.

## Paul's Greeting

> "Paul, Silas and Timothy, To the church of the Thessalonians in God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ: Grace and peace to you from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."

Paul writes with Silas and Timothy.

Grace and peace from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ. These gifts sustain believers through suffering.

**Grace and peace equip us for trials.**

## We Ought Always to Thank God

> "We ought always to thank God for you, brothers and sisters, and rightly so, because your faith is growing more and more, and the love all of you have for one another is increasing."

Thanksgiving for spiritual growth.

Their faith is growing. Their love is increasing. This is evidence of God's work among them.

**Spiritual growth is cause for thanksgiving.**

## We Boast About Your Perseverance

> "Therefore, among God's churches we boast about your perseverance and faith in all the persecutions and trials you are enduring."

The Thessalonians endure persecution.

Paul boasts about their perseverance and faith. They stand firm despite trials.

**Perseverance under persecution glorifies God.**

## Evidence That God's Judgment Is Right

> "All this is evidence that God's judgment is right, and as a result you will be counted worthy of the kingdom of God, for which you are suffering."

Their suffering is evidence of God's righteous judgment.

Not that God is punishing them, but that their faithfulness under trial proves the genuineness of their faith.

They will be counted worthy of the kingdom because they suffer for it.

**Suffering for the kingdom proves genuine faith.**

## God Will Pay Back Trouble to Those Who Trouble You

> "God is just: He will pay back trouble to those who trouble you and give relief to you who are troubled, and to us as well."

God's justice is certain.

He will repay those who persecute His people. He will give relief to the afflicted.

This is not vengeance but vindication. God rights all wrongs.

**God will vindicate His people and judge their persecutors.**

## When the Lord Jesus Is Revealed

> "This will happen when the Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven in blazing fire with his powerful angels. He will punish those who do not know God and do not obey the gospel of our Lord Jesus."

Christ's return will be in glory and judgment.

He will come with blazing fire and powerful angels. He will punish those who do not know God and do not obey the gospel.

**Christ's return brings judgment on unbelievers.**

## They Will Be Punished With Everlasting Destruction

> "They will be punished with everlasting destruction and shut out from the presence of the Lord and from the glory of his might."

Eternal punishment is real.

Everlasting destruction. Shut out from the presence of the Lord. This is the fate of those who reject the gospel.

**Eternal destruction awaits those who reject Christ.**

## On the Day He Comes to Be Glorified

> "On the day he comes to be glorified in his holy people and to be marveled at among all those who have believed."

Christ will be glorified in His people.

The day of His coming is the day of His glorification. Believers will marvel at Him.

**Christ will be glorified in His people at His return.**

## With This in Mind, We Constantly Pray

> "With this in mind, we constantly pray for you, that our God may make you worthy of his calling, and that by his power he may bring to fruition your every desire for goodness and your every deed prompted by faith."

Paul prays for their worthiness.

Not that they earn salvation, but that God makes them worthy of their calling. That He brings to fruition every good desire and work of faith.

**God makes us worthy of His calling by His power.**

## So That the Name of Our Lord Jesus May Be Glorified

> "We pray this so that the name of our Lord Jesus may be glorified in you, and you in him, according to the grace of our God and the Lord Jesus Christ."

The goal is Christ's glory.

That His name be glorified in them, and they in Him. All according to grace.

**The goal of all Christian living is Christ's glory.**

## Final Exhortation

Second Thessalonians 1 encourages believers facing persecution.

Their faith is growing. Their love is increasing. They persevere under trials.

God's judgment is righteous. He will vindicate His people and punish their persecutors when Christ returns in blazing fire.

Eternal destruction awaits those who reject the gospel. But Christ will be glorified in His people.

Paul prays that God will make them worthy of His calling and bring to fruition every work of faith.

The goal is Christ's glory. Persevere in faith.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, strengthen us to persevere under persecution. Thank You for the promise that You will vindicate Your people and judge those who reject the gospel. Make us worthy of Your calling and bring to fruition every work of faith. May Christ be glorified in us. In Jesus' name, amen.
