# Set Your Minds Above: The New Life in Christ

Union with Christ is not just positional. It is transformational.

Paul has established the supremacy and sufficiency of Christ. Now he turns to the ethical implications. If you have been raised with Christ, live like it.

> "Since, then, you have been raised with Christ, set your hearts on things above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things above, not on earthly things."

## Raised with Christ, Hidden with Christ

You have been raised with Christ. This is past tense. Completed action. Your resurrection life has already begun.

Christ is seated at the right hand of God, the place of authority and victory. And you are hidden with Christ in God.

**Your life is secure, concealed in the eternal safety of God's presence.**

When Christ, who is your life, appears, you will also appear with Him in glory. Your identity is tied to His. Your future is guaranteed by His.

This is the foundation for Christian ethics: you are united to the risen, reigning, returning Christ.

## Put to Death What Belongs to Your Earthly Nature

> "Put to death, therefore, whatever belongs to your earthly nature: sexual immorality, impurity, lust, evil desires and greed, which is idolatry."

If you have died with Christ, put to death the deeds of the flesh.

This is not passive. This is active warfare. Kill sin before it kills you.

Sexual immorality, impurity, lust, greed—these are not minor vices. They are idolatry. They elevate creation above the Creator. They worship the gift instead of the Giver.

**Because of these, the wrath of God is coming.**

Do not tolerate sin. Do not make peace with it. Do not excuse it as weakness. Put it to death.

## Take Off the Old Self, Put On the New

> "Do not lie to each other, since you have taken off your old self with its practices and have put on the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge in the image of its Creator."

The old self has been crucified with Christ. It is dead. Do not resurrect it.

Put on the new self, which is being renewed in the image of God. This is progressive sanctification. Daily transformation into Christlikeness.

The image that was marred by sin is being restored in redemption.

**Here there is no Gentile or Jew, circumcised or uncircumcised, barbarian, Scythian, slave or free, but Christ is all, and is in all.**

In Christ, ethnic, religious, cultural, and social distinctions lose their divisive power. Not because they cease to exist, but because Christ is supreme over all.

## Clothe Yourselves with Compassion

> "Therefore, as God's chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience."

Notice the foundation: you are chosen, holy, dearly loved.

Christian ethics flow from identity. You do not act compassionately to become loved. You act compassionately because you are loved.

Compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness, patience—these are not natural virtues. They are fruit of the Spirit. They reflect the character of Christ.

Bear with one another. Forgive one another. Just as the Lord forgave you, so you also must forgive.

**Forgiveness is not optional. It is commanded.**

## Above All, Put On Love

> "And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity."

Love is the outer garment that holds all the virtues together.

Without love, patience becomes cold endurance. Without love, kindness becomes manipulation. Without love, humility becomes self-righteousness.

**Love is the supreme virtue because God is love.**

Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts. Let the message of Christ dwell among you richly. Teach and admonish one another with all wisdom through psalms, hymns, and songs from the Spirit.

And whatever you do, in word or deed, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through Him.

## Household Instructions

> "Wives, submit yourselves to your husbands, as is fitting in the Lord. Husbands, love your wives and do not be harsh with them."

Paul addresses the household: wives, husbands, children, fathers, slaves, masters.

These instructions are grounded in the Lordship of Christ. Submission is "in the Lord." Love reflects Christ's love for the church. Obedience is "in everything, for this pleases the Lord."

**Every relationship is transformed by union with Christ.**

Fathers, do not embitter your children, or they will become discouraged. Masters, provide what is right and fair, knowing you also have a Master in heaven.

The Christian household is not about domination. It is about mutual service under the authority of Christ.

## Work for the Lord

> "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters, since you know that you will receive an inheritance from the Lord as a reward. It is the Lord Christ you are serving."

This transforms mundane labor into worship.

You are not ultimately serving human employers. You are serving Christ. Every task, no matter how small, is done for His glory.

And the reward is eternal: an inheritance from the Lord.

**This dignifies every form of honest labor and frees workers from resentment and employers from exploitation.**

Anyone who does wrong will be repaid for their wrongs, and there is no favoritism.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, You have raised us with Christ and hidden our lives in Him. Teach us to set our minds on things above, to put to death the deeds of the flesh, and to clothe ourselves with love. May our lives reflect the new creation You are making us to be. In Jesus' name, amen.
