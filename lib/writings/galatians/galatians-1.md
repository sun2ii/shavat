# Gospel Defense: No Other Gospel

Galatians is Paul's most urgent letter.

The gospel is under attack. False teachers have infiltrated the churches. Believers are deserting grace for law.

Paul writes with apostolic authority and pastoral passion to defend the gospel of grace.

## Paul's Apostleship

> "Paul, an apostle—sent not from men nor by a man, but by Jesus Christ and God the Father, who raised him from the dead."

Paul asserts his authority immediately.

He is an apostle, not by human appointment but by divine calling. Jesus Christ and God the Father commissioned him.

This matters because false teachers questioned his authority. If Paul's apostleship is illegitimate, his gospel can be dismissed.

**Paul's authority comes from God, not man.**

## No Other Gospel

> "I am astonished that you are so quickly deserting the one who called you to live in the grace of Christ and are turning to a different gospel—which is really no gospel at all."

Paul is astonished.

The Galatians are deserting grace. They are turning to a different gospel. But it is no gospel at all.

There is only one gospel: salvation by grace through faith in Christ alone. Any alteration is a perversion.

**There is no other gospel.**

## Let Him Be Under God's Curse

> "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach a gospel other than the one we preached to you, let them be under God's curse!"

The stakes could not be higher.

If Paul himself or even an angel preached a different gospel, let him be accursed. This is anathema: eternal condemnation.

Paul repeats it twice for emphasis. The gospel is non-negotiable.

**Anyone who preaches a false gospel is under God's curse.**

## Am I Now Trying to Win the Approval of Human Beings?

> "Am I now trying to win the approval of human beings, or of God? Or am I trying to please people? If I were still trying to please people, I would not be a servant of Christ."

Paul does not seek human approval.

Pleasing people and serving Christ are incompatible. The true gospel offends human pride. It eliminates all boasting. It demands total dependence on grace.

**You cannot serve Christ and seek human approval.**

## Paul's Gospel Came by Revelation

> "I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that the gospel I preached is not of human origin. I did not receive it from any man, nor was I taught it; rather, I received it by revelation from Jesus Christ."

Paul's gospel was not learned from men.

He did not study under the apostles. He did not construct it from Scripture. He received it by direct revelation from Jesus Christ.

This validates his message. It is divine, not human.

**The gospel comes by revelation, not human invention.**

## Paul's Former Life

> "For you have heard of my previous way of life in Judaism, how intensely I persecuted the church of God and tried to destroy it. I was advancing in Judaism beyond many of my own age among my people and was extremely zealous for the traditions of my fathers."

Paul's credentials in Judaism were impeccable.

He advanced beyond his peers. He was zealous for the law. He persecuted the church with violent intensity.

But all this was before Christ confronted him.

**Paul's former life was marked by zeal for the law and persecution of the church.**

## God Set Me Apart

> "But when God, who set me apart from my mother's womb and called me by his grace, was pleased to reveal his Son in me so that I might preach him among the Gentiles, my immediate response was not to consult any human being."

God set Paul apart before birth.

This was not random. God's sovereign purpose ordained Paul's calling from the womb. When the time came, God revealed His Son to Paul.

The result: Paul preached Christ among the Gentiles.

**God sets apart whom He will for His purposes.**

## I Did Not Consult with Anyone

> "I did not go up to Jerusalem to see those who were apostles before I was, but I went into Arabia. Later I returned to Damascus."

Paul did not immediately consult the Jerusalem apostles.

He went to Arabia. He spent time alone with God. His gospel came by revelation, not by instruction from men.

This proves the independence and divine origin of his message.

**Paul's gospel was received directly from Christ.**

## After Three Years

> "Then after three years, I went up to Jerusalem to get acquainted with Cephas and stayed with him fifteen days. I saw none of the other apostles—only James, the Lord's brother."

Three years after his conversion, Paul visited Jerusalem.

He spent fifteen days with Peter. He saw James. But this was not to receive the gospel. It was to become acquainted with the other apostles.

Paul's gospel was already complete.

**Paul's gospel was independent of the Jerusalem apostles.**

## I Was Unknown to the Churches in Judea

> "I was personally unknown to the churches of Judea that are in Christ. They only heard the report: 'The man who formerly persecuted us is now preaching the faith he once tried to destroy.' And they praised God because of me."

The churches in Judea did not know Paul personally.

They only heard reports: the persecutor had become a preacher. The destroyer had become a builder.

And they glorified God.

**God's transforming grace brings glory to Himself.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 1 establishes the foundation: the gospel is non-negotiable.

There is no other gospel. Anyone who preaches a false gospel is accursed. Paul's authority is divine, not human. His message came by revelation from Jesus Christ.

Do not desert grace for law. Do not turn from Christ to works. Do not compromise the gospel for the approval of men.

The gospel is this: salvation by grace through faith in Christ alone.

Defend it. Proclaim it. Live it.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for the gospel of grace. Keep us from deserting the truth for a different gospel. Give us boldness to proclaim Christ alone as Savior, and deliver us from the desire to please people rather than You. In Jesus' name, amen.
