# Gospel Defense: Justified by Faith Alone

Paul continues defending the gospel by recounting his interactions with the Jerusalem apostles and his confrontation with Peter.

The issue is clear: are we justified by faith in Christ or by works of the law?

## Fourteen Years Later

> "Then after fourteen years, I went up again to Jerusalem, this time with Barnabas. I took Titus along also. I went in response to a revelation and, meeting privately with those esteemed as leaders, I presented to them the gospel that I preach among the Gentiles."

Fourteen years after his first visit, Paul returned to Jerusalem.

He brought Barnabas and Titus. He met privately with the leaders and presented his gospel to ensure he was not running in vain.

This was not to receive approval for a new message but to confirm unity in the one gospel.

**Paul presented his gospel to the Jerusalem leaders.**

## Not Even Titus Was Compelled to Be Circumcised

> "Yet not even Titus, who was with me and is a Greek, was compelled to be circumcised, even though some false believers had infiltrated our ranks to spy on the freedom we have in Christ Jesus and to make us slaves."

Titus, a Greek, was not required to be circumcised.

This was a test case. If circumcision were necessary for salvation, Titus would have been compelled to undergo it. But the Jerusalem leaders did not require it.

False believers infiltrated to spy on Christian freedom and impose slavery to the law. Paul did not yield.

**Freedom in Christ cannot be compromised.**

## They Added Nothing to My Message

> "As for those who were held in high esteem—whatever they were makes no difference to me; God does not show favoritism—they added nothing to my message."

The Jerusalem apostles added nothing to Paul's gospel.

They recognized that Paul had been entrusted with the gospel for the uncircumcised, just as Peter had been for the circumcised.

Same gospel, different audiences.

**The gospel is one, whether to Jew or Gentile.**

## James, Cephas, and John Gave Us the Right Hand of Fellowship

> "James, Cephas and John, those esteemed as pillars, gave me and Barnabas the right hand of fellowship when they recognized the grace given to me. They agreed that we should go to the Gentiles, and they to the circumcised."

The pillars of the Jerusalem church recognized God's grace in Paul's ministry.

They extended the right hand of fellowship. They affirmed Paul's apostleship and mission to the Gentiles.

Unity in the gospel was preserved.

**The apostles were united in the one gospel.**

## Paul Opposes Peter

> "When Cephas came to Antioch, I opposed him to his face, because he stood condemned. For before certain men came from James, he used to eat with the Gentiles. But when they arrived, he began to draw back and separate himself from the Gentiles because he was afraid of those who belonged to the circumcision group."

Peter compromised the gospel through his conduct.

At first, he ate with Gentile believers, demonstrating their equality in Christ. But when certain men came from Jerusalem, he withdrew out of fear.

This was hypocrisy. It implied that Gentiles were second-class Christians unless they followed Jewish customs.

Paul confronted him publicly.

**Compromising the gospel in practice is as serious as denying it in word.**

## Even Barnabas Was Led Astray

> "The other Jews joined him in his hypocrisy, so that by their hypocrisy even Barnabas was led astray."

Peter's hypocrisy influenced others.

Even Barnabas, Paul's co-worker, was swept along. This shows the danger of inconsistency: it spreads.

**Hypocrisy in gospel matters leads others astray.**

## Not In Step With the Truth of the Gospel

> "When I saw that they were not acting in line with the truth of the gospel, I said to Cephas in front of them all, 'You are a Jew, yet you live like a Gentile and not like a Jew. How is it, then, that you force Gentiles to follow Jewish customs?'"

Paul confronted Peter publicly because the truth of the gospel was at stake.

Peter lived like a Gentile when convenient but withdrew when pressure came. This sent a false message: Gentiles must become Jews to be fully accepted.

**Actions must align with gospel truth.**

## We Are Justified by Faith

> "We who are Jews by birth and not sinful Gentiles know that a person is not justified by the works of the law, but by faith in Jesus Christ. So we, too, have put our faith in Christ Jesus that we may be justified by faith in Christ and not by the works of the law, because by the works of the law no one will be justified."

This is the gospel in one sentence.

Justification is not by works of the law. It is by faith in Jesus Christ.

Even Jews, who had the law, must be justified by faith. The law cannot justify anyone.

**Justification is by faith in Christ alone, not by works of the law.**

## Christ Died for Nothing?

> "I do not set aside the grace of God, for if righteousness could be gained through the law, Christ died for nothing!"

If the law could justify, Christ's death was unnecessary.

But righteousness cannot be gained through the law. The law reveals sin but cannot remove it. Only Christ's death accomplishes justification.

To add works to faith is to nullify grace and empty the cross of its power.

**If righteousness comes by law, Christ died for nothing.**

## I Have Been Crucified With Christ

> "I have been crucified with Christ and I no longer live, but Christ lives in me. The life I now live in the body, I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me."

Union with Christ means death to the old self.

Paul was crucified with Christ. His old identity, rooted in the law and self-righteousness, died.

Now Christ lives in him. The Christian life is not self-improvement but Christ living through the believer by faith.

**I no longer live, but Christ lives in me.**

## I Do Not Set Aside the Grace of God

> "I do not set aside the grace of God, for if righteousness could be gained through the law, Christ died for nothing!"

To add law to grace is to set grace aside.

Grace means unmerited favor. If righteousness comes by law, then grace is no longer grace. And Christ's death becomes pointless.

This is the error of the Galatians: they were nullifying grace by returning to law.

**Do not set aside the grace of God.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 2 demonstrates the centrality of justification by faith.

Paul opposed Peter because his actions contradicted gospel truth. The gospel demands that we recognize justification is by faith in Christ alone, not by works of the law.

If righteousness could come through the law, Christ died for nothing. But He did not die in vain. His death secured justification for all who believe.

We have been crucified with Christ. We no longer live for ourselves. Christ lives in us, and the life we now live, we live by faith in the Son of God who loved us and gave Himself for us.

Do not set aside grace. Do not return to law. Stand firm in the freedom Christ has won.

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for justifying us by faith in Christ alone. Keep us from setting aside Your grace by adding works to faith. May we live by faith in the Son of God who loved us and gave Himself for us. In Jesus' name, amen.
