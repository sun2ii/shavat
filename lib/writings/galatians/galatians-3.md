# Freedom in Christ: The Spirit Received by Faith

Paul moves from defense to argument.

He appeals to the Galatians' own experience and to Scripture to prove that righteousness comes by faith, not law.

## You Foolish Galatians

> "You foolish Galatians! Who has bewitched you? Before your very eyes Jesus Christ was clearly portrayed as crucified."

Paul's rebuke is sharp.

The Galatians were foolish, not intellectually deficient but spiritually deceived. Someone had bewitched them, leading them away from the clear gospel of Christ crucified.

**To abandon grace for law is spiritual foolishness.**

## Did You Receive the Spirit by Works or by Faith?

> "I would like to learn just one thing from you: Did you receive the Spirit by the works of the law, or by believing what you heard?"

Paul appeals to their experience.

When they came to faith, did they receive the Holy Spirit by keeping the law or by believing the gospel?

The answer is obvious: by faith.

**The Spirit is received by faith, not by works.**

## Are You So Foolish?

> "Are you so foolish? After beginning by means of the Spirit, are you now trying to finish by means of the flesh?"

The Christian life begins with the Spirit.

If it began by grace through faith, why would it continue by human effort? The flesh cannot complete what the Spirit begins.

**What begins by the Spirit is completed by the Spirit, not the flesh.**

## Does God Give You His Spirit by Works or by Faith?

> "So again I ask, does God give you his Spirit and work miracles among you by the works of the law, or by your believing what you heard?"

God's ongoing work among them was by faith, not law.

He gave His Spirit. He worked miracles. All of this came through faith, not through law-keeping.

**God works by faith, not by law.**

## Abraham Believed God

> "So also Abraham 'believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness.'"

Abraham is the model of justification by faith.

He believed God, and it was counted to him as righteousness. Not works, but faith. Not obedience to law, but trust in God's promise.

**Abraham was justified by faith, not works.**

## Those Who Have Faith Are Children of Abraham

> "Understand, then, that those who have faith are children of Abraham."

True descendants of Abraham are not defined by ethnicity but by faith.

Those who believe are Abraham's children. The promise belongs to all who trust God, Jew and Gentile alike.

**The children of Abraham are those who have faith.**

## The Scripture Foresaw Justification by Faith

> "Scripture foresaw that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, and announced the gospel in advance to Abraham: 'All nations will be blessed through you.'"

The gospel was preached to Abraham.

God's plan was always to justify the Gentiles by faith. The promise to Abraham included all nations. This was the gospel in seed form.

**The gospel was announced in advance to Abraham.**

## Those Who Rely on the Law Are Under a Curse

> "For all who rely on the works of the law are under a curse, as it is written: 'Cursed is everyone who does not continue to do everything written in the Book of the Law.'"

The law demands perfect obedience.

To rely on the law is to place yourself under its curse. The law says, "Cursed is everyone who does not continue to do everything written in it."

No one keeps the whole law. Therefore, all who rely on it are cursed.

**Relying on the law brings a curse, not blessing.**

## The Righteous Will Live by Faith

> "Clearly no one who relies on the law is justified before God, because 'the righteous will live by faith.'"

Scripture itself declares justification is by faith.

The righteous live by faith, not by law. This is the consistent testimony of God's Word.

**The righteous will live by faith.**

## Christ Redeemed Us From the Curse

> "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us, for it is written: 'Cursed is everyone who is hung on a pole.'"

Christ bore the curse in our place.

He became a curse by dying on the cross. The law pronounced a curse on anyone hanged on a tree. Christ took that curse upon Himself.

**Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law.**

## The Blessing of Abraham Comes Through Faith

> "He redeemed us in order that the blessing given to Abraham might come to the Gentiles through Christ Jesus, so that by faith we might receive the promise of the Spirit."

The purpose of redemption was to bring Abraham's blessing to the Gentiles.

Through Christ, all nations receive the promise of the Spirit by faith.

**The blessing of Abraham comes to all nations through faith in Christ.**

## The Law Does Not Set Aside the Promise

> "The law, introduced 430 years later, does not set aside the covenant previously established by God and thus do away with the promise."

The promise to Abraham predates the law by 430 years.

The law cannot nullify the promise. God's covenant with Abraham was based on promise, not law. The law came later and served a different purpose.

**The promise cannot be set aside by the law.**

## The Law Was Our Guardian

> "So the law was our guardian until Christ came that we might be justified by faith. Now that this faith has come, we are no longer under a guardian."

The law served as a guardian, a custodian, a tutor.

It confined people under sin, showing them their need for a Savior. But when Christ came, the age of the guardian ended.

Now we are justified by faith, no longer under the law's supervision.

**The law was a guardian until Christ came.**

## You Are All Children of God Through Faith

> "So in Christ Jesus you are all children of God through faith, for all of you who were baptized into Christ have clothed yourselves with Christ."

Sonship comes through faith in Christ.

Baptism into Christ signifies union with Him. Believers have clothed themselves with Christ, taking on His identity.

**You are children of God through faith in Christ.**

## There Is Neither Jew Nor Gentile

> "There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus."

In Christ, earthly distinctions lose their ultimate significance.

Jew and Gentile are one. Slave and free are one. Male and female are one. All are equal before God, justified by the same faith.

**In Christ, all believers are one.**

## If You Belong to Christ, You Are Abraham's Seed

> "If you belong to Christ, then you are Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise."

Union with Christ makes believers heirs of the promise.

Belonging to Christ means belonging to Abraham's line. The promise comes through faith, not through ethnic descent or law-keeping.

**Believers in Christ are heirs of the promise.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 3 demolishes the idea that justification comes by works of the law.

The Galatians received the Spirit by faith, not by works. Abraham was justified by faith. The law brings a curse, but Christ redeemed us from that curse.

The law served as a guardian until Christ came. Now we are justified by faith and are children of God in Christ.

In Christ, all distinctions are transcended. Jew and Gentile are one. All who belong to Christ are Abraham's seed and heirs of the promise.

Do not return to law. Live by faith. You are children of God through faith in Jesus Christ.

## Closing Prayer
