# Freedom in Christ: No Longer Slaves But Sons

Paul deepens his argument by contrasting slavery and sonship.

Before Christ, we were slaves. In Christ, we are sons and heirs.

## Heirs as Children

> "What I am saying is that as long as an heir is underage, he is no different from a slave, although he owns the whole estate."

An underage heir has no practical freedom.

Though he owns the estate, he is under guardians and trustees. His status is potential, not actual.

This illustrates life under the law. The promises belonged to God's people, but they lived in a state of immaturity and restriction.

**Before Christ, we were like underage heirs under guardians.**

## In Slavery Under the Elemental Spiritual Forces

> "So also, when we were underage, we were in slavery under the elemental spiritual forces of the world."

Life under the law was slavery.

Paul calls these "elemental spiritual forces," referring to the basic principles of the world: rules, rituals, external observances.

The law could not liberate. It confined.

**Life under law is slavery to external principles.**

## But When the Set Time Had Fully Come

> "But when the set time had fully come, God sent his Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to redeem those under the law, that we might receive adoption to sonship."

God's timing is perfect.

At the appointed time, He sent His Son. Jesus was born of a woman, fully human. He was born under the law to redeem those under the law.

The purpose: adoption as sons.

**Christ came to redeem us and make us sons.**

## God Sent the Spirit of His Son

> "Because you are his sons, God sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, the Spirit who calls out, 'Abba, Father.'"

Sonship is confirmed by the Spirit.

God sent the Spirit into believers' hearts. The Spirit testifies to our adoption by calling God "Abba, Father."

This is intimate, relational language. Not a distant deity but a loving Father.

**The Spirit testifies that we are sons of God.**

## No Longer a Slave But a Son

> "So you are no longer a slave, but God's child; and since you are his child, God has made you also an heir."

The transformation is complete.

From slave to son. From bondage to freedom. From fear to confidence.

And as sons, we are heirs. We inherit all that belongs to the Father through Christ.

**You are no longer a slave but a son and heir.**

## Turning Back to Weak and Miserable Principles

> "But now that you know God—or rather are known by God—how is it that you are turning back to those weak and miserable forces? Do you wish to be enslaved by them all over again?"

The Galatians knew God. More accurately, God knew them.

Why, then, would they return to bondage? Why trade sonship for slavery? Why go back to weak and worthless principles?

This is spiritual regression, not progress.

**Do not return to the slavery you have been freed from.**

## Paul's Concern for the Galatians

> "I fear for you, that somehow I have wasted my efforts on you."

Paul fears his labor was in vain.

If the Galatians abandon the gospel, his work among them will have been for nothing. This is pastoral anguish, not mere disappointment.

**Apostasy threatens to nullify gospel ministry.**

## Become Like Me

> "I plead with you, brothers and sisters, become like me, for I became like you. You did me no wrong."

Paul became like the Galatians by setting aside Jewish customs to minister among Gentiles.

Now he pleads with them to become like him: free from the law, living by grace through faith.

**Follow the example of those who live by grace.**

## You Welcomed Me as an Angel of God

> "As you know, it was because of an illness that I first preached the gospel to you, and even though my illness was a trial to you, you did not treat me with contempt or scorn. Instead, you welcomed me as if I were an angel of God, as if I were Christ Jesus himself."

When Paul first came, the Galatians received him warmly despite his physical affliction.

They treated him as a messenger from God, even as Christ Himself. Their love was genuine.

**Their initial reception of Paul was marked by love.**

## What Has Happened to Your Joy?

> "What has happened to all your joy? I can testify that, if you could have done so, you would have torn out your eyes and given them to me."

The Galatians' joy has vanished.

They once loved Paul so deeply they would have sacrificed for him. Now false teachers have stolen their joy and turned them against him.

**False teaching destroys joy.**

## Have I Become Your Enemy by Telling You the Truth?

> "Have I now become your enemy by telling you the truth?"

Paul's loyalty to truth has made him seem like an enemy.

But true friends speak truth, even when it is uncomfortable. False teachers flatter and deceive.

**Truth-telling may seem like enmity but is actually love.**

## Those People Are Zealous to Win You Over

> "Those people are zealous to win you over, but for no good. What they want is to alienate you from us, so that you may have zeal for them."

The false teachers are zealous, but their motives are corrupt.

They want to alienate the Galatians from Paul so they can control them. Their goal is not the Galatians' good but their own glory.

**Beware of teachers who seek to control rather than serve.**

## Hagar and Sarah

> "Tell me, you who want to be under the law, are you not aware of what the law says? For it is written that Abraham had two sons, one by the slave woman and the other by the free woman."

Paul uses allegory to contrast law and promise.

Hagar represents slavery and the old covenant. Sarah represents freedom and the new covenant.

Ishmael was born by human effort. Isaac was born by divine promise.

**Law is like Hagar; promise is like Sarah.**

## Children of Promise

> "Now you, brothers and sisters, like Isaac, are children of promise."

Believers are children of promise, not of slavery.

We are not born of human effort but of divine grace. Like Isaac, our birth is miraculous, the result of God's promise.

**You are children of promise, not of the flesh.**

## Persecuted by the Son of the Slave Woman

> "At that time the son born according to the flesh persecuted the son born by the power of the Spirit. It is the same now."

Ishmael persecuted Isaac.

So it is now: those who rely on the flesh persecute those who live by the Spirit. This conflict is inevitable.

**Expect persecution from those who cling to law.**

## Get Rid of the Slave Woman

> "But what does Scripture say? 'Get rid of the slave woman and her son, for the slave woman's son will never share in the inheritance with the free woman's son.'"

There is no coexistence between law and grace.

The slave woman and her son must be cast out. Law cannot share the inheritance with promise.

**You cannot inherit through both law and grace.**

## We Are Children of the Free Woman

> "Therefore, brothers and sisters, we are not children of the slave woman, but of the free woman."

Believers belong to the line of Sarah, not Hagar.

We are free, not slaves. We inherit by promise, not by law.

**We are children of the free woman.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 4 contrasts slavery and sonship.

Before Christ, we were slaves under the law. But when the time had fully come, God sent His Son to redeem us and adopt us as sons.

We are no longer slaves but sons and heirs. The Spirit testifies to our adoption. We call God "Abba, Father."

Do not return to the weak and worthless principles of the world. Do not trade sonship for slavery.

We are children of promise, like Isaac. We are children of the free woman, not the slave.

Stand firm in the freedom Christ has won.

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for adopting us as sons through Christ and sending Your Spirit into our hearts. Keep us from returning to slavery. Help us to live as children of promise, free in Christ. In Jesus' name, amen.
