# Life in the Spirit: Stand Firm in Freedom

Freedom is the theme of Galatians 5.

But freedom is not license. It is freedom from sin to serve God, freedom from law to walk in the Spirit.

## Stand Firm in Freedom

> "It is for freedom that Christ has set us free. Stand firm, then, and do not let yourselves be burdened again by a yoke of slavery."

Christ set us free for freedom.

Not partial freedom. Not freedom from some sins but not others. Complete freedom from the law's condemnation and slavery.

Stand firm. Do not return to bondage. The yoke of slavery has been broken.

**Christ has set us free; do not return to slavery.**

## If You Let Yourselves Be Circumcised

> "Mark my words! I, Paul, tell you that if you let yourselves be circumcised, Christ will be of no value to you at all."

Circumcision itself is not the issue. The issue is what it represents: reliance on law for justification.

If you seek justification through law-keeping, you nullify Christ. Grace and law are incompatible systems. You must choose one.

**If you rely on law, Christ is of no value to you.**

## You Are Obligated to Obey the Whole Law

> "Again I declare to every man who lets himself be circumcised that he is obligated to obey the whole law."

Partial obedience to the law is not an option.

If you choose the law, you must keep all of it. This is impossible. The law demands perfection, and no one attains it.

**The law demands total obedience, which no one can give.**

## You Have Fallen Away From Grace

> "You who are trying to be justified by the law have been alienated from Christ; you have fallen away from grace."

Seeking justification by law is apostasy.

To pursue righteousness through law is to be alienated from Christ and to fall from grace. These are mutually exclusive.

**Justification by law means falling from grace.**

## We Eagerly Await the Righteousness for Which We Hope

> "For through the Spirit we eagerly await by faith the righteousness for which we hope."

Believers wait for the final vindication of righteousness.

Not by works, but by faith through the Spirit. The verdict of "righteous" is already pronounced, but its full manifestation awaits the last day.

**We await the righteousness that comes by faith.**

## In Christ Neither Circumcision Nor Uncircumcision Means Anything

> "For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision has any value. The only thing that counts is faith expressing itself through love."

External rituals have no saving value.

Circumcision does not justify. Uncircumcision does not condemn. What matters is faith working through love.

**Faith expressing itself through love is what counts.**

## A Little Yeast Leavens the Whole Batch

> "You were running a good race. Who cut in on you to keep you from obeying the truth? That kind of persuasion does not come from the one who calls you. A little yeast works through the whole batch of dough."

The Galatians were running well.

But false teachers cut in on them, hindering their progress. This persuasion did not come from God.

A little error spreads quickly. False teaching is like yeast, permeating the whole community.

**Beware of false teaching; it spreads like yeast.**

## You Were Called to Be Free

> "You, my brothers and sisters, were called to be free. But do not use your freedom to indulge the flesh; rather, serve one another humbly in love."

Freedom is not license to sin.

Christian freedom is freedom from the law's condemnation, but it is not freedom to indulge selfish desires. True freedom serves others in love.

**Use your freedom to serve one another in love.**

## Love Fulfills the Law

> "For the entire law is fulfilled in keeping this one command: 'Love your neighbor as yourself.'"

The law's moral essence is love.

All the commandments are summed up in love for neighbor. Love does not violate the law; it fulfills it.

**Love fulfills the whole law.**

## Do Not Bite and Devour Each Other

> "If you bite and devour each other, watch out or you will be destroyed by each other."

Conflict destroys community.

If believers attack one another, they will consume themselves. Unity requires love, not strife.

**Avoid destructive conflict within the church.**

## Walk by the Spirit

> "So I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh."

The solution to sinful desires is walking by the Spirit.

This is not self-effort but dependence on the Spirit's power. The Spirit enables obedience that the flesh cannot produce.

**Walk by the Spirit to overcome the flesh.**

## The Flesh Desires What Is Contrary to the Spirit

> "For the flesh desires what is contrary to the Spirit, and the Spirit what is contrary to the flesh. They are in conflict with each other, so that you are not to do whatever you want."

Flesh and Spirit are at war.

The desires of the flesh oppose the Spirit. The desires of the Spirit oppose the flesh. This conflict is real and ongoing.

**Flesh and Spirit are opposed to each other.**

## If You Are Led by the Spirit, You Are Not Under the Law

> "But if you are led by the Spirit, you are not under the law."

Being led by the Spirit means freedom from the law.

The Spirit produces what the law demands but cannot create: righteousness, love, obedience.

**The Spirit-led life is free from the law.**

## The Works of the Flesh

> "The acts of the flesh are obvious: sexual immorality, impurity and debauchery; idolatry and witchcraft; hatred, discord, jealousy, fits of rage, selfish ambition, dissensions, factions and envy; drunkenness, orgies, and the like."

The flesh produces sin.

Paul lists representative sins: sexual immorality, idolatry, hatred, divisions, envy, drunkenness. These are the fruit of living according to the flesh.

**The flesh produces sinful works.**

## Those Who Live Like This Will Not Inherit the Kingdom

> "I warn you, as I did before, that those who live like this will not inherit the kingdom of God."

Persistent, unrepentant sin excludes from the kingdom.

This is not sinless perfectionism but the pattern of life. Those who habitually practice such things show no evidence of the Spirit's transforming work.

**Persistent sin proves absence of true faith.**

## The Fruit of the Spirit

> "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control. Against such things there is no law."

The Spirit produces Christ-like character.

Love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control. These are the marks of the Spirit's presence.

The law cannot condemn such fruit. It embodies what the law requires.

**The Spirit produces Christ-like fruit.**

## Those Who Belong to Christ Have Crucified the Flesh

> "Those who belong to Christ Jesus have crucified the flesh with its passions and desires."

Union with Christ means death to the flesh.

Believers have crucified the sinful nature. This is positional truth (already accomplished in Christ) and practical reality (to be worked out daily).

**The flesh is crucified in Christ.**

## Since We Live by the Spirit, Let Us Keep in Step With the Spirit

> "Since we live by the Spirit, let us keep in step with the Spirit."

Life in the Spirit demands walking in step with the Spirit.

This is active cooperation with the Spirit's leading. Not passive waiting but intentional following.

**Keep in step with the Spirit.**

## Let Us Not Become Conceited

> "Let us not become conceited, provoking and envying each other."

Pride destroys unity.

Conceit leads to provocation and envy. The Spirit produces humility, not pride. Walk in love, not in selfish ambition.

**Avoid conceit; walk in humility.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 5 presents the life of Christian freedom.

Christ has set us free. Stand firm in that freedom. Do not return to slavery under the law. Do not use freedom as license for sin. Serve one another in love.

Walk by the Spirit, not by the flesh. The flesh produces sin. The Spirit produces Christ-like character: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control.

Those who belong to Christ have crucified the flesh. Live by the Spirit. Keep in step with the Spirit.

This is true freedom: freedom from sin, freedom to serve, freedom to walk in love.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for setting us free in Christ. Help us to stand firm in that freedom and not return to slavery. Teach us to walk by the Spirit, producing the fruit of love, joy, peace, and self-control. In Jesus' name, amen.
