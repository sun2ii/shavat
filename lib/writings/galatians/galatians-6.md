# Life in the Spirit: Sow to the Spirit, Reap Eternal Life

Galatians concludes with practical exhortations for life in the Spirit.

How do believers walk in freedom? By bearing burdens, sowing to the Spirit, and boasting only in the cross.

## Restore One Another Gently

> "Brothers and sisters, if someone is caught in a sin, you who live by the Spirit should restore that person gently. But watch yourselves, or you also may be tempted."

When a believer falls into sin, restoration is the goal.

Those who are spiritual should restore the person gently, not harshly. Humility is essential because anyone can fall into temptation.

**Restore sinning believers gently, with humility.**

## Carry Each Other's Burdens

> "Carry each other's burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ."

Love bears others' burdens.

The law of Christ is the law of love. Bearing burdens fulfills this law. We are called to share in one another's struggles, not to isolate ourselves.

**Carry each other's burdens.**

## If Anyone Thinks They Are Something

> "If anyone thinks they are something when they are nothing, they deceive themselves."

Pride is self-deception.

No one is self-sufficient. Thinking you are something when you are nothing is delusion. Humility sees reality: we are dependent on God and one another.

**Pride is self-deception; walk in humility.**

## Each One Should Test Their Own Actions

> "Each one should test their own actions. Then they can take pride in themselves alone, without comparing themselves to someone else, for each one should carry their own load."

Self-examination is necessary.

Test your own actions, not someone else's. Take satisfaction in your own obedience without comparing yourself to others.

While we bear one another's burdens, each person is responsible for their own conduct.

**Test your own actions; carry your own load.**

## Share All Good Things

> "Nevertheless, the one who receives instruction in the word should share all good things with the one who teaches."

Those who are taught should support those who teach.

This is a principle of reciprocity. Teachers give spiritual instruction. Learners give material support.

**Support those who teach the Word.**

## Do Not Be Deceived: God Cannot Be Mocked

> "Do not be deceived: God cannot be mocked. A man reaps what he sows."

There are spiritual laws of cause and effect.

God is not mocked. You cannot sow one thing and reap another. Actions have consequences.

**You reap what you sow.**

## Sow to the Flesh, Reap Destruction

> "Whoever sows to please their flesh, from the flesh will reap destruction; whoever sows to please the Spirit, from the Spirit will reap eternal life."

Two paths, two outcomes.

Sowing to the flesh leads to destruction. Sowing to the Spirit leads to eternal life.

This is not salvation by works but evidence of true faith. What you sow reveals where your heart is.

**Sowing to the flesh leads to destruction; sowing to the Spirit leads to life.**

## Let Us Not Become Weary in Doing Good

> "Let us not become weary in doing good, for at the proper time we will reap a harvest if we do not give up."

Perseverance is essential.

Do not grow weary in doing good. The harvest is coming, but it requires endurance. Do not give up.

**Do not grow weary in doing good; the harvest is coming.**

## Do Good to All People

> "Therefore, as we have opportunity, let us do good to all people, especially to those who belong to the family of believers."

Good works extend to all.

Prioritize the household of faith, but do good to everyone. This is the outworking of love.

**Do good to all, especially to fellow believers.**

## Those Who Want to Impress People

> "Those who want to impress people by means of the flesh are trying to compel you to be circumcised. The only reason they do this is to avoid being persecuted for the cross of Christ."

False teachers seek human approval.

They compel circumcision not because it is necessary for salvation but because it avoids persecution. The cross offends. Compromise with the world avoids conflict.

**False teachers seek to avoid the offense of the cross.**

## Not Even Those Who Are Circumcised Keep the Law

> "Not even those who are circumcised keep the law, yet they want you to be circumcised that they may boast about your circumcision in the flesh."

The false teachers are hypocrites.

They do not keep the law themselves. They want others circumcised so they can boast in their influence and numbers.

**False teachers are hypocrites who do not keep the law themselves.**

## May I Never Boast Except in the Cross

> "May I never boast except in the cross of our Lord Jesus Christ, through which the world has been crucified to me, and I to the world."

Paul's only boast is the cross.

Not in accomplishments. Not in converts. Not in status. Only in the cross of Jesus Christ.

Through the cross, the world is crucified to Paul, and Paul to the world. The values of the world hold no appeal.

**Boast only in the cross of Christ.**

## Neither Circumcision Nor Uncircumcision Means Anything

> "Neither circumcision nor uncircumcision means anything; what counts is the new creation."

External rituals are irrelevant.

What matters is the new creation. Those who are in Christ are new creatures. Old things have passed away; all things have become new.

**What counts is the new creation in Christ.**

## Peace and Mercy to All Who Follow This Rule

> "Peace and mercy to all who follow this rule—to the Israel of God."

Peace and mercy belong to those who live by this gospel.

The Israel of God is not ethnic Israel but all who are justified by faith, the true children of Abraham.

**Peace and mercy to the Israel of God.**

## I Bear on My Body the Marks of Jesus

> "From now on, let no one cause me trouble, for I bear on my body the marks of Jesus."

Paul's scars testify to his suffering for Christ.

The marks of Jesus are the wounds from persecution, beatings, and hardship endured for the gospel. These authenticate his ministry.

**Paul bears the marks of Jesus on his body.**

## The Grace of Our Lord Jesus Christ Be With Your Spirit

> "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit, brothers and sisters. Amen."

The letter ends with grace.

It began with grace and ends with grace. The gospel is grace from beginning to end.

**Grace be with your spirit.**

## Final Exhortation

Galatians 6 concludes the letter with practical wisdom for life in the Spirit.

Restore sinning believers gently. Carry each other's burdens. Test your own actions. Do not be deceived: you reap what you sow.

Sow to the Spirit, not to the flesh. Do not grow weary in doing good. The harvest is coming.

Do not seek to impress people or avoid persecution. Boast only in the cross. What counts is the new creation.

Paul bears the marks of Jesus on his body. His scars testify to his faithfulness.

And the letter ends where it began: with grace.

The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, help us to sow to the Spirit and not to the flesh. Give us strength to carry each other's burdens and to persevere in doing good. May we boast only in the cross of Christ and live as new creations. Grace be with our spirits. In Jesus' name, amen.
