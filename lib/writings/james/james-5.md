# Faith That Works: Patient Endurance Until Christ Returns

James 5 concludes the letter with warnings, exhortations, and promises.

Judgment is coming for oppressors. Patience is required of believers. Prayer is powerful. And restoration is the church's responsibility.

This is not abstract theology. This is practical wisdom for those waiting for Christ's return.

## Judgment on the rich

> "Now listen, you rich people, weep and wail because of the misery that is coming on you."

James pronounces judgment on the wealthy who have hoarded, exploited, and murdered.

Their wealth has rotted. Moths have eaten their clothes. Gold and silver are corroded. And their corrosion testifies against them.

Stored wealth is temporary and corruptible. What seems secure is already destroyed.

> "Look! The wages you failed to pay the workers who mowed your fields are crying out against you. The cries of the harvesters have reached the ears of the Lord Almighty."

Oppression of workers is heard by God.

Withheld wages cry out. The cries of the exploited reach the Lord of Hosts. God is the defender of the oppressed.

> "You have lived on earth in luxury and self-indulgence. You have fattened yourselves in the day of slaughter."

Like cattle fattened for slaughter, the rich are unknowingly preparing for judgment.

Indulgence blinds to impending destruction. They have condemned and murdered the innocent, who did not resist.

**Judgment is coming. The misery of the rich will be great.**

## Be patient until the Lord's coming

> "Be patient, then, brothers and sisters, until the Lord's coming. See how the farmer waits for the land to yield its valuable crop, patiently waiting for the autumn and spring rains."

Patience is required.

The farmer waits for the crop. He cannot rush the process. He patiently expects the rain.

So believers must wait for Christ's return. Patience is not passivity. It is active endurance, trusting that God will bring His purposes to completion.

> "You too, be patient and stand firm, because the Lord's coming is near."

Stand firm. The Lord's return is imminent. His coming fuels perseverance.

> "Don't grumble against one another, brothers and sisters, or you will be judged. The Judge is standing at the door!"

Do not grumble under pressure. The Judge is near. Internal conflict is sin when judgment is imminent.

**Patience with God does not excuse impatience with others.**

## Examples of perseverance

> "Brothers and sisters, as an example of patience in the face of suffering, take the prophets who spoke in the name of the Lord."

The prophets suffered yet remained faithful. Their endurance is a model.

> "As you know, we count as blessed those who have persevered. You have heard of Job's perseverance and have seen what the Lord finally brought about. The Lord is full of compassion and mercy."

Job is the exemplar of patient endurance.

He suffered greatly yet trusted God. And God's purpose in suffering was ultimately good. The Lord is compassionate and merciful.

**Suffering is temporary. God's mercy is eternal.**

## Let your yes be yes

> "Above all, my brothers and sisters, do not swear—not by heaven or by earth or by anything else. All you need to say is a simple 'Yes' or 'No.' Otherwise you will be condemned."

Echoes of Jesus' teaching in Matthew 5:33-37.

Oaths indicate distrust. Simple, honest speech should suffice. Integrity makes oaths unnecessary.

**Your word should be trustworthy without embellishment.**

## The prayer of faith

> "Is anyone among you in trouble? Let them pray. Is anyone happy? Let them sing songs of praise."

Prayer and praise are appropriate responses to all circumstances.

Trouble drives us to God. Joy overflows in worship. Life circumstances should lead to God, not away from Him.

> "Is anyone among you sick? Let them call the elders of the church to pray over them and anoint them with oil in the name of the Lord."

Corporate prayer for the sick. Anointing with oil may be medicinal or symbolic.

Church leadership is involved in care for the sick. Prayer is the primary action.

> "And the prayer offered in faith will make the sick person well; the Lord will raise them up. If they have sinned, they will be forgiven."

Faith-filled prayer leads to healing and forgiveness.

Sickness and sin are sometimes connected, but not always. God is sovereign in healing.

> "Therefore confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective."

Mutual confession and intercession. Prayer is not passive but dynamic, effective.

**Righteousness enables powerful prayer.**

## Elijah's example

> "Elijah was a human being, even as we are. He prayed earnestly that it would not rain, and it did not rain on the land for three and a half years. Again he prayed, and the heavens gave rain, and the earth produced its crops."

Elijah as example of effective prayer.

He was ordinary, yet his prayers had extraordinary power. Faithfulness, not superhuman ability, makes prayer effective.

God responds to the prayers of the faithful. Prayer is powerful because God is powerful.

**The righteous have access to divine power through prayer.**

## Restore the wandering

> "My brothers and sisters, if one of you should wander from the truth and someone should bring that person back, remember this: Whoever turns a sinner from the error of their way will save them from death and cover over a multitude of sins."

Restoration is communal responsibility.

Wandering from truth is spiritual danger. The church must pursue straying members.

Turning a sinner from error saves from death and covers a multitude of sins. Evangelism and discipleship are acts of love that save lives.

**Faith works by restoring the fallen.**

## Final exhortation

James 5 calls us to patient endurance, fervent prayer, and faithful restoration.

Judgment is coming for oppressors. Believers must wait patiently for Christ's return, stand firm under pressure, and trust in God's compassion.

Prayer is powerful. Confess your sins. Pray for one another. Seek God in trouble and joy.

And restore those who wander. Bringing a sinner back from error is an act of love that saves lives.

This is faith that works: patient, prayerful, and restorative.

Do you endure trials with patience?

Do you pray with faith?

Do you pursue the wandering?

**This is what faith looks like when it is alive.**

---

## Closing prayer

Father, give us patience to endure until Christ returns. Make us fervent in prayer and faithful in restoring those who wander. May our lives demonstrate faith that works through love, endurance, and intercession. In Jesus' name, amen.
