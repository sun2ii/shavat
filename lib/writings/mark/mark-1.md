# The Servant Son of God: The Beginning of the Gospel

Mark does not waste a single word.

He does not begin with a genealogy. He does not begin with a birth narrative. He begins with a thunderclap:

> "The beginning of the gospel of Jesus Christ, the Son of God."

In one sentence, Mark gives us everything. Jesus is the Christ — the promised Messiah. Jesus is the Son of God — equal with the Father. And what unfolds is not a biography. It is gospel. Good news.

A servant does not need a pedigree. Mark introduces Jesus by what He does.

## The voice in the wilderness

> "Prepare the way of the Lord, make his paths straight."

John the Baptist arrives in camel's hair, eating locusts, preaching repentance. He is the herald clearing the road for the King who comes as a Servant.

John is not the light. He points to the Light. He is not the Word. He prepares for the Word.

**True greatness in the Kingdom always points away from itself to Christ.**

## The Father speaks at the Jordan

Jesus comes from Nazareth and is baptized by John. As He comes up from the water:

> "You are my beloved Son; with you I am well pleased."

The heavens are torn open. The Spirit descends like a dove. The Father speaks audibly.

This is the divine endorsement of the Servant Son. Before He has performed a single miracle, before He has preached a single sermon, the Father declares His pleasure.

The Servant is loved before He serves. The Son is approved before He suffers.

## Tested in the wilderness

Immediately the Spirit drives Jesus into the wilderness. Forty days. Tempted by Satan. Among the wild beasts. Attended by angels.

Mark gives us almost no detail. He does not pause for the dialogue Matthew and Luke record. Mark is fast. He wants us to see the action.

The Servant Son enters the field of battle. He resists where Adam fell. He overcomes where Israel failed. The wilderness has met its Master.

## The Kingdom is at hand

After John is arrested, Jesus comes preaching:

> "The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand; repent and believe in the gospel."

This is the Servant's first sermon. Four phrases. The whole gospel in miniature.

The time is fulfilled — God's clock has struck the hour.

The Kingdom is at hand — the reign of God has come near in the person of Christ.

Repent — turn from sin.

Believe — trust the good news.

**The Kingdom is not earned. It is announced. And those who hear must repent and believe.**

## Follow Me

He walks by the Sea of Galilee. He sees Simon and Andrew casting nets. He sees James and John mending nets.

> "Follow me, and I will make you become fishers of men."

Immediately they leave the nets. Immediately they leave the boat. Immediately they leave their father.

Mark uses the word *immediately* over and over. The Servant moves. His call demands response. There is no slow deliberation when the King passes by.

## Authority in the synagogue

In Capernaum on the Sabbath, Jesus enters the synagogue and teaches. The crowds are astonished:

> "He taught them as one who had authority, and not as the scribes."

The scribes quoted other scribes. Jesus spoke as the source of truth itself.

A man with an unclean spirit cries out. Jesus rebukes the demon: "Be silent, and come out of him." The demon obeys.

**The Servant Son has authority over the unseen world. No darkness can stand when He speaks.**

## A servant who heals and prays

That evening they bring Him the sick and the demon-possessed. The whole city gathers at the door. He heals many. He casts out many.

And then — long before dawn — He rises and goes out to a desolate place to pray.

The Servant does not run on adrenaline. He runs on communion with the Father. The busiest day is followed by the earliest morning of prayer.

Then He moves on. He will not stay where the crowds want Him. He came to preach in many towns.

## A leper made clean

A leper kneels before Him. "If you will, you can make me clean."

> "Moved with pity, he stretched out his hand and touched him and said, 'I will; be clean.'"

The Servant touches the untouchable. The Holy One contacts the unclean — and instead of becoming defiled, He cleanses. This is the gospel in one gesture.

**The Servant Son does not keep His distance. He stretches out His hand and heals.**

---

## Closing prayer

Father, thank You for sending Your beloved Son as the Servant who serves and the King who reigns. Open our ears to hear His voice — "Follow Me" — and give us grace to leave our nets and follow without delay. Touch us with His authority, cleanse us with His mercy, and make us witnesses of the gospel that begins and ends in Him. In Jesus' name, amen.
