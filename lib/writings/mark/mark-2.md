# The Servant Son of God: Authority to Forgive Sins

Mark 2 shows the Servant doing what only God can do.

A paralyzed man is lowered through a roof. Four friends have torn the tiles to get him close to Jesus. Mark is not interested in the architecture. He is interested in what the friends did and what Jesus said.

> "When Jesus saw their faith, he said to the paralytic, 'Son, your sins are forgiven.'"

The crowd came for a miracle. Jesus addresses the deeper wound first.

## The deeper sickness

The man's body is broken. His soul is broken. Jesus speaks to the soul.

This is the order of the gospel. **Sin is the disease beneath every disease.** Death is the symptom; sin is the cause.

The Servant does not flatter the man. He does not promise health and prosperity. He forgives sins — because that is the deepest need any of us will ever have.

## The scribes accuse Him of blasphemy

The scribes reason in their hearts: *Who can forgive sins but God alone?*

They are right about the doctrine. Only God can forgive sins.

What they refuse to see is the conclusion: if Jesus forgives sins, Jesus is God.

> "Why is it easier to say 'Your sins are forgiven' or to say 'Rise, take up your bed and walk'? But that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins…"

He commands the man to rise. The man rises. The crowd is amazed.

**The miracle of healing is the proof of the miracle of forgiveness.** The visible verifies the invisible.

## The call of Levi

Then Jesus walks past a tax booth. Levi sits there — a Jew collecting taxes for Rome, hated by his own people, profiting from oppression.

> "Follow me."

Levi rises and follows.

He throws a feast. Tax collectors and sinners crowd his table. Jesus reclines among them.

The Pharisees are scandalized. The Servant Son is unmoved. He is exactly where He came to be.

## The Physician's reply

> "Those who are well have no need of a physician, but those who are sick. I came not to call the righteous, but sinners."

This is one of the great gospel verses. Jesus did not come to certify the self-righteous. He came to call sinners.

If you think you are well, you will not come to the Physician. If you know you are sick, you will run to Him.

**The door of the Kingdom opens to those who know they are not well.**

## A question about fasting

The disciples of John fast. The Pharisees fast. Jesus' disciples do not. Why?

> "Can the wedding guests fast while the bridegroom is with them?"

This is staggering. Jesus calls Himself the Bridegroom. In the Old Testament, the Bridegroom is the Lord Himself.

His presence is the wedding feast. To fast in the presence of the Bridegroom would be to misread the moment. The day will come when He is taken away — and then they will fast.

## New wine and old wineskins

> "No one puts new wine into old wineskins."

The Servant did not come to patch up the old system. He came to inaugurate something new — a new covenant in His blood.

Religion that tries to bottle Jesus inside old wineskins will burst. The gospel does not fit inside legalism. The Servant Son breaks the molds we try to put Him in.

## Lord of the Sabbath

In the grainfields, the disciples pluck heads of grain on the Sabbath. The Pharisees object.

Jesus reminds them of David eating the consecrated bread. Then He delivers the verdict:

> "The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath. So the Son of Man is lord even of the Sabbath."

The Servant is greater than the Sabbath. The Sabbath was given for human good. And the One who gave it is here.

**Jesus does not bow to the rules of men. The rules bow to Him.**

---

## Closing prayer

Father, thank You that the Servant Son has authority on earth to forgive sins. We come as the paralyzed, the tax collectors, the sinners — knowing we are not well, knowing only Christ can heal. Forgive us, raise us up, and seat us at the Bridegroom's table. Save us from religion that bottles You in old wineskins and grant us the new wine of the gospel. In Jesus' name, amen.
