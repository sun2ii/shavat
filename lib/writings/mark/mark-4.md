# The Servant Son of God: The Word and the Wind

Mark 4 puts two pictures side by side.

In the first half, the Servant teaches in parables — the Word goes out as seed. In the second half, He silences a storm at sea with a word — the same Word that scatters seed commands the wind.

The same voice. The same authority. Once over hearts, once over waves.

## The parable of the sower

The crowd is so large that Jesus gets into a boat and teaches them from the water. He begins with a story everyone in an agricultural society would understand.

A sower goes out to sow. The seed falls in four places.

- The path — the birds eat it.
- The rocky soil — it springs up quickly but withers in the sun.
- The thorny soil — it grows but is choked.
- The good soil — it bears fruit, thirty, sixty, a hundredfold.

Then Jesus says:

> "He who has ears to hear, let him hear."

The story is simple. The application is searching.

## Why parables?

The disciples ask. Jesus answers:

> "To you has been given the secret of the kingdom of God, but for those outside everything is in parables."

Parables are not mere illustrations. They reveal to those who lean in, and they conceal from those who turn away. They are mercy and judgment in the same words.

**The Servant Son speaks in a way that exposes the hearers. The same Word will save one and harden another.**

## The interpretation

Jesus explains the parable Himself. The seed is the Word. The soils are hearts.

The path is the hard heart — Satan snatches the Word before it can take root.

The rocky soil is the shallow heart — initial joy, no endurance, falls away under pressure.

The thorny soil is the divided heart — the cares of the world, the deceitfulness of riches, the desires for other things choke the Word.

The good soil is the receiving heart — hears, accepts, bears fruit.

The same seed. The same Sower. The difference is the soil.

**Examine your heart. The Word is being sown. Is it bearing fruit?**

## A lamp on a stand

He gives a small parable: a lamp is not lit to be put under a basket. It is lit to give light.

> "For nothing is hidden except to be made manifest."

The Kingdom looks small now. The Word looks weak now. But what is concealed will be revealed. Truth always emerges.

The Servant Son will not stay hidden. The Kingdom He plants will be seen by the whole world.

## The seed grows secretly

Another short parable: a man scatters seed and goes to sleep. The seed sprouts and grows. He does not know how.

> "The earth produces by itself."

This is a deep comfort. **The Kingdom grows by the inner power of the Word, not by the cleverness of the sower.** We sow. God gives the growth.

The Servant's Kingdom advances even while we sleep.

## The mustard seed

The smallest of seeds becomes the largest of garden plants. Birds make nests in its branches.

This is the trajectory of the Kingdom. It begins almost invisibly — a Servant from Nazareth, twelve unlikely men, a crucified Messiah. It ends with every tribe and tongue gathered to Him.

**Do not despise small beginnings. The mustard seed becomes a tree.**

## Storm on the sea

Evening comes. Jesus says: "Let us go across to the other side."

A great windstorm arises. The waves break into the boat. The boat is filling. Jesus is asleep on the cushion.

The disciples panic. They wake Him:

> "Teacher, do you not care that we are perishing?"

He awakes. He rebukes the wind. He says to the sea:

> "Peace! Be still!"

The wind ceases. There is a great calm.

Then He turns to them: "Why are you so afraid? Have you still no faith?"

## Who then is this?

The disciples are filled with great fear:

> "Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?"

This is the question Mark is driving toward. The answer has already been given in chapter 1: He is the Son of God.

Only God commands the sea. In the Psalms it is the LORD who stills the storm. Jesus speaks one word and the elements obey.

**The same Word that sows the Kingdom in hearts is the Word that rules the wind.** The Servant Son is no mere teacher. He is the Lord of creation, asleep in your boat, ready to speak peace when you call.

---

## Closing prayer

Father, sow Your Word in the soil of our hearts and let it bear fruit thirty, sixty, a hundredfold. Save us from the hard path, the shallow rock, the choking thorns. And when the storm rises and the waves break in, give us faith to wake the Servant Son who is in the boat — knowing that one word from Him stills every sea. In Jesus' name, amen.
