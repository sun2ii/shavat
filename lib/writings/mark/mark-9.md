# The Servant Son of God: Glory Unveiled, Cross Foretold

Mark 9 holds two mountains and a valley.

On one mountain, Jesus is transfigured — for a moment His true glory shines through the veil of the Servant's flesh. In the valley, He confronts a demon His disciples could not cast out. And as the chapter unfolds, He keeps teaching the lesson the disciples cannot grasp: the way of the Servant Son is the way of the cross, and the way of greatness is the way of the child.

Glory and humility are not opposites in this Servant. They are the same path.

## The Transfiguration

Six days after Peter's confession, Jesus takes Peter, James, and John up a high mountain.

> "He was transfigured before them, and his clothes became radiant, intensely white, as no one on earth could bleach them."

The glory that is always His shines through. Moses and Elijah appear with Him — the Law and the Prophets, both pointing to Him.

Peter, not knowing what to say, blurts:

> "Rabbi, it is good that we are here. Let us make three tents…"

He wants to freeze the moment. He puts Jesus on the same level as Moses and Elijah.

Then a cloud overshadows them, and the voice of the Father speaks:

> "This is my beloved Son; listen to him."

When they look, only Jesus is there. Moses is gone. Elijah is gone. Only Jesus.

**The Father will not let Christ be ranked with the prophets.** He stands alone. Listen to Him.

## Tell no one — yet

Coming down the mountain, Jesus charges them to tell no one what they have seen until the Son of Man has risen from the dead.

They keep the matter to themselves, questioning what "rising from the dead" might mean. They ask Him about Elijah coming first.

He confirms: Elijah has already come, in John the Baptist. They did to him whatever they pleased.

And the Son of Man? He must suffer many things and be treated with contempt.

**The Transfiguration was given as fuel for the dark days ahead.** What you have seen of His glory on the mountain must hold you when you stand at the foot of the cross.

## The demon the disciples could not cast out

They come down to a crowd. A great argument is going on. The other disciples could not cast out a spirit from a man's son.

The father pleads with Jesus: "If you can do anything, have compassion on us and help us."

Jesus answers:

> "'If you can'! All things are possible for one who believes."

The father cries out with one of the most honest prayers in all of Scripture:

> "I believe; help my unbelief!"

This is the prayer of every disciple. Faith and weakness in the same breath. The Servant Son does not turn him away. He casts out the demon. He takes the boy by the hand and lifts him up.

In private, the disciples ask why they could not cast it out. He tells them:

> "This kind cannot be driven out by anything but prayer."

Some battles in the Kingdom yield only to prayer. The Servant Son did not deal with darkness on His own strength alone — He prayed. And neither will we.

## The second prediction of the cross

Passing through Galilee, He teaches:

> "The Son of Man is going to be delivered into the hands of men, and they will kill him. And when he is killed, after three days he will rise."

They do not understand. They are afraid to ask.

But on the way, they have been arguing — about which of them is the greatest.

The Servant has just told them He is going to die. They are arguing about rank.

**This is the chronic disease of the human heart, even the disciple's heart: position-seeking in the shadow of the cross.**

## A child in His arms

When they reach Capernaum, He asks what they were discussing. They are silent — they are ashamed.

He sits down, calls the twelve, and says:

> "If anyone would be first, he must be last of all and servant of all."

Then He takes a child in His arms — a child, the lowest in the social order — and says:

> "Whoever receives one such child in my name receives me, and whoever receives me, receives not me but him who sent me."

The Servant Son measures greatness by who serves the weakest. To welcome the unimportant person in His name is to welcome Him. To welcome Him is to welcome the Father.

**There is no shortcut to greatness in the Kingdom.** The way up is the way down.

## "Whoever is not against us is for us"

John reports: "Teacher, we saw someone casting out demons in your name, and we tried to stop him."

Jesus answers:

> "Do not stop him… For the one who is not against us is for us."

The disciples wanted exclusivity. Jesus is broader than their categories. Anyone genuinely working in His name is on His side.

## Severe warnings about sin

Then He gives some of the sternest words in the Gospels:

> "Whoever causes one of these little ones who believe in me to sin, it would be better for him if a great millstone were hung around his neck and he were thrown into the sea."

If your hand causes you to sin, cut it off. If your foot, cut it off. If your eye, tear it out.

He is not commanding self-mutilation. He is commanding radical seriousness about sin. Whatever is destroying your soul, get rid of it — no matter how dear, no matter how costly. **Hell is real, and nothing in this world is worth keeping if it costs you eternity with Christ.**

## Salt and peace

He closes with a strange image: every one will be salted with fire. Salt is good — but if salt loses its saltiness, what is it good for?

> "Have salt in yourselves, and be at peace with one another."

The Servant Son ends the chapter where He began the discussion of greatness: not in arguing, but in peace. The disciples of the Christ do not jockey for position. They have salt — distinctive holiness — and peace with one another.

---

## Closing prayer

Father, lift the veil and let us see the glory of Your beloved Son — and then steady our hearts to follow Him to the cross. We confess with the father in the crowd: we believe; help our unbelief. Tear out of us every ambition for rank, and put a child in our arms in Your name. Make us last of all and servants of all, for the sake of the Servant Son. In Jesus' name, amen.
