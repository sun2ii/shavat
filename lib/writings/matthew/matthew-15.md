# The Promised King: Mercy Reaches the Gentiles

Matthew 15 puts two scenes side by side that show the King redrawing the lines.

The Pharisees from Jerusalem confront Him about His disciples eating with unwashed hands. He answers by exposing how their tradition has nullified the command of God. He teaches: it is not what enters the mouth that defiles, but what comes out of the heart.

Then He withdraws to the region of Tyre and Sidon — Gentile territory — and meets a Canaanite woman whose daughter is demon-possessed. Her faith is so striking that He marvels at it.

He returns to the region of the Decapolis. Great crowds gather. He heals them all. He feeds four thousand from seven loaves and a few small fish.

The Promised King is widening His mercy. **What He has done for Israel He is now doing for the Gentiles too.**

## Tradition and the heart

Then Pharisees and scribes came to Jesus from Jerusalem:

> "Why do your disciples break the tradition of the elders? For they do not wash their hands when they eat."

This was not a hygiene question. It was about the ritual washing that the rabbis had added to the Law over centuries.

He answered them:

> "And why do you break the commandment of God for the sake of your tradition?"

He gives an example. The Law commanded honor of father and mother. But the Pharisees had developed a rule whereby a man could declare his resources *Corban* — devoted to God — and refuse to use them to help his aging parents.

Religion had invented a loophole around the Fifth Commandment.

> "So for the sake of your tradition you have made void the word of God. You hypocrites! Well did Isaiah prophesy of you, when he said: 'This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me. In vain do they worship me, teaching as doctrines the commandments of men.'"

**The Promised King exposes religion that uses God's words to disobey God's commands.** It is one of the deepest forms of hypocrisy — and one of the most common.

## What defiles

He called the people to Him:

> "Hear and understand: it is not what goes into the mouth that defiles a person, but what comes out of the mouth; this defiles a person."

The disciples come and say: *Do you know that the Pharisees were offended when they heard this saying?*

He answers:

> "Every plant that my heavenly Father has not planted will be rooted up. Leave them; they are blind guides. And if the blind lead the blind, both will fall into a pit."

Peter asks Him to explain the parable.

He says:

> "Are you also still without understanding? Do you not see that whatever goes into the mouth passes into the stomach and is expelled? But what comes out of the mouth proceeds from the heart, and this defiles a person."

> "For out of the heart come evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false witness, slander. These are what defile a person. But to eat with unwashed hands does not defile anyone."

**The heart is the problem. The mouth only reveals it.** Sin is not a soap-and-water problem. The heart itself is corrupt. Only the Promised King can change it.

## A Canaanite woman

Jesus went away from there and withdrew to the district of Tyre and Sidon — Gentile territory.

Behold, a Canaanite woman from that region came out and was crying:

> "Have mercy on me, O Lord, Son of David; my daughter is severely oppressed by a demon."

She uses the Jewish messianic title — *Son of David.* She knows who He is.

But He did not answer her a word. His disciples came and begged Him: *Send her away, for she is crying out after us.*

He answered:

> "I was sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Israel."

She came and knelt before Him: *Lord, help me.*

He answered:

> "It is not right to take the children's bread and throw it to the dogs."

This sounds harsh. The Greek word is a softened term — *little dogs*, household pets, not feral curs. But still — the King is testing her faith with the apparent order of His mission: to Israel first.

She does not get offended. She does not despair. She turns His picture into a plea:

> "Yes, Lord; yet even the dogs eat the crumbs that fall from their masters' table."

This is one of the most stunning answers in the Gospels. She accepts the picture. She does not demand the children's bread. She asks only for the crumbs.

Jesus answers:

> "O woman, great is your faith! Be it done for you as you desire."

Her daughter was healed instantly.

**The Promised King marvels at the faith of a Gentile woman who insists on the crumbs of mercy.** He has called the faith of only two people *great* in the Gospels — a Roman centurion and a Canaanite woman. Both were Gentiles. Both refused to be turned away.

## The four thousand fed

Jesus went on from there and walked beside the Sea of Galilee. He went up on the mountain and sat down there. Great crowds came to Him, bringing the lame, the blind, the crippled, the mute, and many others. They put them at His feet, and He healed them. The crowd wondered, when they saw the mute speaking, the crippled healthy, the lame walking, and the blind seeing. And they glorified the God of Israel.

He called His disciples and said:

> "I have compassion on the crowd because they have been with me now three days and have nothing to eat. And I am unwilling to send them away hungry, lest they faint on the way."

The disciples said: *Where are we to get enough bread in such a desolate place to feed so great a crowd?*

Jesus said: *How many loaves do you have?*

They said: *Seven, and a few small fish.*

He directed the crowd to sit down on the ground. He took the seven loaves and the fish, gave thanks, broke them, and gave them to the disciples, and the disciples gave them to the crowds. They all ate and were satisfied. They took up seven baskets full of the broken pieces left over.

Those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.

**The Promised King feeds the crowd a second time.** Five thousand fed in Jewish territory. Four thousand fed near Gentile country. The miracle is repeated — partly because the disciples had not learned from the first, and partly because the King's bread is for both Jew and Gentile.

The bread that satisfied the five thousand fills the four thousand too. The Kingdom of the Promised King is large enough for all who come.

---

## Closing prayer

Father, save us from religion that nullifies Your Word for the sake of human tradition. Cleanse not just our hands but our hearts, from which all true defilement flows. Give us the Canaanite woman's faith — to insist on the crumbs of mercy, knowing that the King's table is large enough even for us. And let us be among the crowd that has been fed and satisfied by His hand. In Jesus' name, amen.
