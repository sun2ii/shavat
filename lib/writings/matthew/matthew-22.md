# The Promised King: The King Silences His Opponents

Matthew 22 is the day the King silenced every faction that came against Him.

He tells the parable of the wedding feast — where those invited refused to come, so the king sent his servants to the highways to invite everyone. The Pharisees and Herodians try to trap Him on taxes. The Sadducees try to trap Him on the resurrection. A lawyer asks Him the greatest commandment. Finally, He poses His own question, about how the Christ can be both David's son and David's Lord.

By the end of the chapter, no one dares to ask Him another question.

The Promised King stands above every faction. Every test designed to catch Him only catches the testers.

## The wedding feast

Again Jesus spoke to them in parables:

> "The kingdom of heaven may be compared to a king who gave a wedding feast for his son, and sent his servants to call those who were invited to the wedding feast, but they would not come."

He sent other servants: *Tell those who are invited, "See, I have prepared my dinner, my oxen and my fat calves have been slaughtered, and everything is ready. Come to the wedding feast."*

But they paid no attention. One went to his farm, another to his business. The rest seized his servants, treated them shamefully, and killed them.

The king was angry. He sent his troops and destroyed those murderers and burned their city.

Then he said to his servants:

> "The wedding feast is ready, but those invited were not worthy. Go therefore to the main roads and invite to the wedding feast as many as you find."

The servants went out and gathered all whom they found, both bad and good. The wedding hall was filled with guests.

**The Promised King opens His Father's feast to anyone who will come.** The expected guests refused. So the table is filled from the highways.

## The man without a wedding garment

But when the king came in to look at the guests, he saw a man who had no wedding garment. He said: *Friend, how did you get in here without a wedding garment?* The man was speechless.

The king said: *Bind him hand and foot and cast him into the outer darkness. In that place there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.*

> "For many are called, but few are chosen."

**Coming in is not enough. You must come on the King's terms — clothed in the garment He provides.** That garment is the righteousness of Christ. To try to enter the wedding in your own clothes is to be exposed at the door.

## A question about taxes

Then the Pharisees went and plotted how to entangle Him in His words. They sent their disciples to Him, along with the Herodians:

> "Teacher, we know that you are true and teach the way of God truthfully, and you do not care about anyone's opinion, for you are not swayed by appearances. Tell us, then, what you think. Is it lawful to pay taxes to Caesar, or not?"

This is a perfect trap. If He says yes, He alienates the patriots. If He says no, He is a rebel.

Jesus, aware of their malice, said:

> "Why put me to the test, you hypocrites? Show me the coin for the tax."

They brought Him a denarius. He said:

> "Whose likeness and inscription is this?"

They said: *Caesar's.*

He said:

> "Therefore render to Caesar the things that are Caesar's, and to God the things that are God's."

When they heard it, they marveled. They left Him and went away.

**The Promised King's answer cuts both ways.** The coin bears Caesar's image — pay him his tax. You bear God's image — give Him yourself entirely.

## The Sadducees on the resurrection

The same day Sadducees came to Him, who say there is no resurrection. They asked Him about a hypothetical: a woman married seven brothers in succession. Whose wife will she be in the resurrection?

Jesus answered:

> "You are wrong, because you know neither the Scriptures nor the power of God. For in the resurrection they neither marry nor are given in marriage, but are like angels in heaven."

> "And as for the resurrection of the dead, have you not read what was said to you by God: 'I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob'? He is not God of the dead, but of the living."

When the crowd heard it, they were astonished at His teaching.

**The Promised King grounds the resurrection in the very identity of God.** The patriarchs are alive to God. The resurrection is the consequence of God's eternal covenant with His people.

## The greatest commandment

When the Pharisees heard that He had silenced the Sadducees, they gathered together. One of them, a lawyer, asked Him a question to test Him:

> "Teacher, which is the great commandment in the Law?"

He said:

> "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment. And a second is like it: You shall love your neighbor as yourself. On these two commandments depend all the Law and the Prophets."

**The Promised King reduces the whole Law to two commandments.** Love God. Love neighbor. Every other command is a specification of these.

These are not two commandments separable from each other. The first produces the second. You cannot truly love God without loving the neighbor He has made in His image.

## David's son and David's Lord

Now while the Pharisees were gathered together, Jesus asked them a question:

> "What do you think about the Christ? Whose son is he?"

They said: *The son of David.*

He said:

> "How is it then that David, in the Spirit, calls him Lord, saying, 'The Lord said to my Lord, "Sit at my right hand, until I put your enemies under your feet"'? If then David calls him Lord, how is he his son?"

No one was able to answer Him a word. From that day, no one dared to ask Him any more questions.

The Christ is both the Son of David and the Lord of David. He is the descendant of the great king — and infinitely more. **He is fully Son of David and fully Son of God.**

The Pharisees cannot answer. To answer would require them to confess what stood before them: that Jesus is the divine Christ. They refuse. They are silent.

The chapter ends with the King unbroken — and His opponents silenced.

## Closing prayer

Father, thank You that the Promised King silenced every test brought against Him — by Pharisees, by Herodians, by Sadducees, by lawyers. Make us those who come to Your wedding feast clothed in the righteousness of Your Son. Render us wholly to You as those who bear Your image. Love You with all our heart, soul, and mind, and love our neighbor as ourselves. And worship the Christ who is both David's Son and David's Lord. In Jesus' name, amen.
