# The Promised King: The Herald of the King

Matthew 3 introduces the man who runs ahead of the King.

John the Baptist appears in the wilderness of Judea, dressed in camel's hair, eating locusts and wild honey, calling the nation to repent. He is not a polished preacher. He does not flatter his hearers. He calls them a brood of vipers and tells them the axe is already at the root of the trees.

Then, into the crowd at the Jordan, comes the King Himself — and John baptizes Him.

The Promised King comes not in a chariot of fire but in line with sinners at the Jordan. The voice of the Father speaks. The Spirit descends. The Trinity is openly present at the inauguration of His ministry.

## John in the wilderness

> "In those days John the Baptist came preaching in the wilderness of Judea: 'Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand.'"

This is the keynote of Matthew's Gospel: *the kingdom of heaven is at hand.* The reign of God is no longer far off. It is breaking in. The Promised King is on His way.

John is the herald. Heralds in the ancient world preceded the king's arrival, announcing his coming, preparing the road, demanding the welcome.

Matthew identifies him as the fulfillment of Isaiah:

> "The voice of one crying in the wilderness: 'Prepare the way of the Lord, make his paths straight.'"

**The Promised King has a herald. Repentance is the way the road is prepared.**

## A wild prophet

John wears a garment of camel's hair and a leather belt around his waist. His food is locusts and wild honey.

He is dressed like Elijah. He eats what the wilderness provides. He is not a smooth-tongued teacher in fine robes. He is a prophet from another era, calling Israel back to her covenant.

Then Jerusalem and all Judea and all the region about the Jordan go out to him. They are baptized by him in the river Jordan, confessing their sins.

**A great crowd is admitting in public what they have hidden in private.** This is the work of the Spirit through the herald: people who would have died in their sins are now confessing them.

## "You brood of vipers"

When John sees many of the Pharisees and Sadducees coming for baptism, he does not soften the message:

> "You brood of vipers! Who warned you to flee from the wrath to come? Bear fruit in keeping with repentance. And do not presume to say to yourselves, 'We have Abraham as our father,' for I tell you, God is able from these stones to raise up children for Abraham."

He shatters two of their deepest assumptions:

- That they were safe because of their ancestry.
- That outward religious form was the same as inward repentance.

Heritage saves no one. **The Promised King does not measure people by their family tree but by the fruit of repentance.**

> "Even now the axe is laid to the root of the trees. Every tree therefore that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire."

The judgment is imminent. The time for fruit is now.

## "He who is coming after me"

John points beyond himself:

> "I baptize you with water for repentance, but he who is coming after me is mightier than I, whose sandals I am not worthy to carry. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and fire. His winnowing fork is in his hand, and he will clear his threshing floor and gather his wheat into the barn, but the chaff he will burn with unquenchable fire."

The herald is not the King. The voice is not the Word. John's baptism is water. The King's baptism is Spirit and fire — purifying His own and judging the chaff.

**The Promised King is the divider — wheat from chaff, true from false, those who repent from those who refuse.** John does not soften this. The herald who has called for repentance now tells them what the King will do when He arrives.

## The King steps into the Jordan

Then Jesus comes from Galilee to the Jordan to John, to be baptized by him.

John tries to prevent Him:

> "I need to be baptized by you, and do you come to me?"

The herald recognizes the King. He knows his place. The Servant is greater than the messenger.

But Jesus answers:

> "Let it be so now, for thus it is fitting for us to fulfill all righteousness."

This is one of the most important lines in the Gospel.

Why is the sinless King baptized with a baptism of repentance? Not for His own sins — He had none. He is baptized in solidarity with sinners. He stands in their line. He takes His place among them. He identifies fully with the people He came to save.

**The Promised King's whole ministry begins with this gesture: He stands with sinners, takes their place, fulfills the righteousness they could not fulfill themselves.**

This is the gospel in one act — six weeks before His first sermon, three years before the cross. Christ steps into the river and takes the sinner's place.

## The heavens open

> "And when Jesus was baptized, immediately he went up from the water, and behold, the heavens were opened to him, and he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and coming to rest on him; and behold, a voice from heaven said, 'This is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased.'"

This is the Trinity revealed.

- The Son is in the water.
- The Spirit descends on Him.
- The Father speaks from heaven.

The Father declares two things: *This is my beloved Son.* — *With whom I am well pleased.*

The first establishes who Jesus is. The second establishes the Father's delight in Him.

**The Promised King is not approved because of what He has done — He has not yet preached a sermon or performed a miracle. He is approved because of who He is.** Loved before He served. Pleasing before He suffered. The Father's delight comes first.

And this is the gospel for us. By faith in Christ, we are united to Him. The Father's verdict on Him becomes the Father's verdict on us: *beloved. Well pleased.*

## The King ready for His mission

The King has been confirmed. The herald has done his work. The heavens have opened. The Spirit rests on Him. The Father has spoken.

Now the King turns toward the wilderness — for there is one more test before His public ministry begins. Where Israel failed, He must succeed. Where Adam fell, He must stand.

The Promised King is ready. The world does not yet know what is coming. But the chapter ends with everything in place: the herald, the river, the dove, the voice, the King.

## Closing prayer

Father, thank You for the herald who prepared the way — and for the King who stepped into the river to fulfill all righteousness. Thank You for the voice that spoke over Him — *beloved Son, well pleased* — and that, by faith, that same word is spoken over us. Make us a people who bear fruit in keeping with repentance. Strip from us every false confidence in heritage or form, and let us belong to the Promised King by grace alone. In Jesus' name, amen.
