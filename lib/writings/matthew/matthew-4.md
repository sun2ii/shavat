# The Promised King: The King Resists the Usurper

Matthew 4 is the showdown.

The Promised King has been baptized and confirmed by the Father. The Spirit has descended. Now the same Spirit drives Him into the wilderness, to be tested by the devil.

Forty days of fasting. Three temptations. Three times the King answers from Scripture. Three times the usurper is defeated.

Then the King comes out of the wilderness, calls His first disciples, and begins to preach the message that will define His mission:

> "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."

Where the first Adam fell to the serpent in a garden, the new Adam stands in a desert. Where Israel failed in the wilderness for forty years, Christ succeeds in forty days. The Promised King resists the usurper and inaugurates His reign.

## Led by the Spirit

> "Then Jesus was led up by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted by the devil."

This is not Satan dragging Christ off to a place of weakness. This is the Spirit leading the King to a place of testing.

He fasts forty days and forty nights. And afterward He is hungry.

Then the tempter comes.

## "If you are the Son of God"

> "If you are the Son of God, command these stones to become loaves of bread."

The Father had just declared *This is my beloved Son.* The devil's first move is to put a *if* in front of that declaration.

He does not deny that Jesus is the Son. He attacks the implications. If you really are the Son, then You should not be hungry. The Father is starving You. Take matters into Your own hands.

Jesus answers from Deuteronomy 8 — the chapter where Moses reminded Israel of God's provision of manna in the wilderness:

> "It is written, 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God.'"

**The Promised King will not break His fast on the devil's terms.** He will wait for the Father's provision. Bread matters — but God's word matters more.

## "Throw yourself down"

The devil takes Him to the holy city and sets Him on the pinnacle of the temple:

> "If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down, for it is written, 'He will command his angels concerning you,' and 'On their hands they will bear you up, lest you strike your foot against a stone.'"

The devil has learned to quote Scripture. He cites Psalm 91. He misuses it to suggest that the Father has promised dramatic deliverance whenever the Son tests Him.

Jesus answers, again from Deuteronomy:

> "Again it is written, 'You shall not put the Lord your God to the test.'"

**The Promised King will not manipulate the Father into a display.** Faith does not demand spectacle. Faith trusts where it cannot see.

The devil quotes Scripture out of context. Jesus answers with Scripture in context. **The only safe way to handle the Bible is to handle the whole Bible — not isolated verses ripped from their place.**

## "All these will I give you"

The devil takes Him to a very high mountain and shows Him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory:

> "All these I will give you, if you will fall down and worship me."

This is the offer of the kingdoms without the cross.

Jesus has come to receive every kingdom — but by way of Calvary. The devil offers a shortcut. Bow once. Skip the suffering. Take the world.

Jesus answers:

> "Be gone, Satan! For it is written, 'You shall worship the Lord your God and him only shall you serve.'"

**The Promised King will not accept the kingdoms by a shortcut that bypasses the Father's will.** He will receive them — but only by laying down His life.

The devil leaves Him. Angels come and minister to Him.

The wilderness is finished. The first Adam fell. The new Adam stands.

## "The land of Zebulun and Naphtali"

When Jesus hears that John has been arrested, He withdraws into Galilee. Leaving Nazareth, He goes and lives in Capernaum by the sea, in the territory of Zebulun and Naphtali.

This fulfills Isaiah:

> "The land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles — the people dwelling in darkness have seen a great light, and for those dwelling in the region and shadow of death, on them a light has dawned."

**The Promised King chooses to base His ministry in Galilee — a Gentile-mixed region in the north, despised by sophisticated Jerusalem.** He goes to the people in darkness. He moves to the shadowed places. He shines first where people most needed light.

## "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand"

From that time Jesus begins to preach:

> "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand."

This is His first sermon. It is the same message John had been preaching. The herald and the King speak the same word — because the message of repentance is what the King's coming requires.

The Kingdom is not future. It is here. It has come in His person. The right response is to turn.

## "Follow me"

Walking by the Sea of Galilee, He sees two brothers, Simon called Peter and Andrew his brother, casting a net into the sea. They are fishermen.

> "Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men."

Immediately they leave their nets and follow Him.

Going on from there, He sees two other brothers, James the son of Zebedee and John his brother, in the boat with Zebedee their father, mending their nets. He calls them. Immediately they leave the boat and their father and follow Him.

Two pairs of brothers. Four fishermen. The King is gathering His court — and He gathers from the docks, not the palaces.

**The Promised King does not pick the obvious candidates.** He picks rough men by the sea and turns them into fishers of souls.

## The King's ministry begins

He goes throughout all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the gospel of the kingdom, and healing every disease and every affliction among the people.

His fame spreads throughout all Syria. They bring Him all the sick — those afflicted with various diseases and pains, those oppressed by demons, epileptics, and paralytics — and He heals them.

Great crowds follow Him from Galilee and the Decapolis, from Jerusalem and Judea, and from beyond the Jordan.

The Kingdom has arrived. The King is in motion. The crowd is gathering. The mission has begun.

**Where the King goes, light comes — and darkness yields.**

## Closing prayer

Father, thank You that the Promised King resisted every temptation that the first Adam fell to and Israel failed against. Thank You that He answered the usurper with Your Word. Teach us to live by every word that comes from Your mouth, not to put You to the test, and to worship and serve You only. Call us, as You called the fishermen, with one word — *follow me* — and give us grace to leave our nets immediately. In Jesus' name, amen.
