# Righteousness and Peace: Counting All as Loss for Christ

Paul shifts from exhortation to autobiography, from doctrine to testimony.

But the testimony serves a purpose: to demonstrate what it means to find all value in Christ alone.

## Rejoice in the Lord

> "Further, my brothers and sisters, rejoice in the Lord! It is no trouble for me to write the same things to you again, and it is a safeguard for you."

Rejoice in the Lord. Not in circumstances. Not in achievements. In the Lord.

Repetition is not burden but safeguard. Paul does not tire of calling believers to joy because joy anchors faith.

**Rejoice in the Lord always.**

## Beware of Dogs

> "Watch out for those dogs, those evildoers, those mutilators of the flesh. For it is we who are the circumcision, we who serve God by his Spirit, who boast in Christ Jesus, and who put no confidence in the flesh."

Dogs, evildoers, mutilators.

Paul's language is harsh because the threat is severe. False teachers insisted on circumcision for salvation, adding works to grace.

True circumcision is of the heart, by the Spirit. True worship boasts in Christ, not in flesh. The people of God are defined by faith, not by ritual.

**Put no confidence in the flesh.**

## Paul's Former Confidence in the Flesh

> "If someone else thinks they have reasons to put confidence in the flesh, I have more: circumcised on the eighth day, of the people of Israel, of the tribe of Benjamin, a Hebrew of Hebrews; in regard to the law, a Pharisee; as for zeal, persecuting the church; as for righteousness based on the law, faultless."

Paul's pedigree was impeccable.

Circumcised on the eighth day. Pure Israelite lineage. Tribe of Benjamin. Hebrew of Hebrews. Pharisee. Zealous persecutor. Legally faultless.

If anyone could boast in the flesh, it was Paul.

**Paul had every human credential.**

## Counting All as Loss

> "But whatever were gains to me I now consider loss for the sake of Christ."

Radical revaluation.

What Paul once counted as gain, he now counts as loss. His credentials became liabilities. His achievements became obstacles.

Why? For the sake of Christ.

**Everything that competed with Christ became loss.**

## Surpassing Worth of Knowing Christ

> "What is more, I consider everything a loss because of the surpassing worth of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord, for whose sake I have lost all things. I consider them garbage, that I may gain Christ."

Everything is loss compared to knowing Christ.

Not just religious achievements. Everything. All things. Paul calls them garbage, refuse, dung. Not worthless in themselves, but worthless compared to Christ.

Knowing Christ surpasses all. This is not mere information but intimate relationship.

**The surpassing worth of knowing Christ relativizes all else.**

## Not Having a Righteousness of My Own

> "And be found in him, not having a righteousness of my own that comes from the law, but that which is through faith in Christ—the righteousness that comes from God on the basis of faith."

Two righteousnesses.

One from the law: human effort, self-achievement, external obedience. Paul had this. It was insufficient.

One from God: received by faith, granted through Christ, perfect and complete. This is the righteousness Paul sought.

**True righteousness comes from God by faith, not from works.**

## Knowing Christ

> "I want to know Christ—yes, to know the power of his resurrection and participation in his sufferings, becoming like him in his death."

Knowing Christ is threefold.

First, the power of His resurrection. Life from death. Divine power transforming the believer.

Second, participation in His sufferings. Fellowship in affliction. Sharing His rejection and pain.

Third, becoming like Him in death. Self-denial, crucifixion of the flesh, dying to self.

**Knowing Christ means resurrection power and suffering fellowship.**

## Not Yet Perfect

> "Not that I have already obtained all this, or have already arrived at my goal, but I press on to take hold of that for which Christ Jesus took hold of me."

Paul has not arrived. He is not yet perfect.

But he presses on. He pursues. He strains forward. Why? Because Christ took hold of him for this purpose.

**Press on toward the goal, even though you have not arrived.**

## Forgetting What is Behind

> "Brothers and sisters, I do not consider myself yet to have taken hold of it. But one thing I do: Forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead, I press on toward the goal to win the prize for which God has called me heavenward in Christ Jesus."

One thing: press on.

Forget what is behind. Do not dwell on past achievements. Do not rest on former obedience. Do not be paralyzed by past failures.

Strain toward what is ahead. Run the race. Pursue the goal. The prize is the upward call of God in Christ.

**Forget what is behind; press toward the goal ahead.**

## Follow Paul's Example

> "Join together in following my example, brothers and sisters, and just as you have us as a model, keep your eyes on those who live as we do."

Imitation is central to discipleship.

Follow Paul's example. Watch those who live this way. Patterns of godliness are learned through observation and imitation.

**Follow the example of those who pursue Christ.**

## Enemies of the Cross

> "For, as I have often told you before and now tell you again even with tears, many live as enemies of the cross of Christ. Their destiny is destruction, their god is their stomach, and their glory is in their shame. Their mind is set on earthly things."

Not all who profess Christ follow Him.

Enemies of the cross pursue earthly things. Their god is appetite. Their glory is shame. They set their minds on temporal pleasures.

Paul speaks with tears. False teachers are dangerous, and their end is destruction.

**Beware those whose minds are set on earthly things.**

## Our Citizenship is in Heaven

> "But our citizenship is in heaven. And we eagerly await a Savior from there, the Lord Jesus Christ, who, by the power that enables him to bring everything under his control, will transform our lowly bodies so that they will be like his glorious body."

Believers are citizens of heaven.

This world is not our home. We await a Savior from heaven: the Lord Jesus Christ.

He will transform our lowly bodies to be like His glorious body. Resurrection is coming. Transformation is guaranteed.

**Our citizenship is in heaven; we await the Savior.**

## Final Exhortation

Philippians 3 calls for single-minded devotion to Christ.

Count all things as loss for His surpassing worth. Abandon confidence in the flesh. Pursue the righteousness that comes from God by faith.

Know Christ in resurrection power and suffering fellowship. Press on toward the goal, forgetting what is behind. Follow godly examples. Avoid those whose minds are set on earthly things.

Our citizenship is in heaven. We await the Savior who will transform us into His likeness.

This is the heart of Christian life: knowing Christ and being found in Him.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, give us hearts that count all things as loss for the surpassing worth of knowing Christ. Deliver us from confidence in the flesh. Help us press on toward the goal, forgetting what is behind and straining toward what is ahead. May we live as citizens of heaven, eagerly awaiting our Savior. In Jesus' name, amen.
