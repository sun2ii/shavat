# Sound Doctrine: Appoint Faithful Elders

Titus is a pastoral epistle, written to guide a young leader in establishing churches on the island of Crete.

The focus is clear: sound doctrine, godly leadership, and transformed living.

## Paul's Greeting

> "Paul, a servant of God and an apostle of Jesus Christ to further the faith of God's elect and their knowledge of the truth that leads to godliness."

Paul serves to further faith and knowledge.

Not just any knowledge, but knowledge of the truth that leads to godliness. Truth is not abstract. It produces transformation.

**True knowledge leads to godliness.**

## The Hope of Eternal Life

> "Faith and knowledge resting on the hope of eternal life, which God, who does not lie, promised before the beginning of time."

Christian faith rests on hope.

Eternal life was promised before time began. God cannot lie. His promise is absolutely certain.

**Our hope rests on God's promise of eternal life.**

## Appoint Elders in Every Town

> "The reason I left you in Crete was that you might put in order what was left unfinished and appoint elders in every town, as I directed you."

Titus's mission is to establish order.

Appoint elders in every town. This is essential for church health. Leadership must be established according to biblical qualifications.

**Godly leadership is essential for church order.**

## Qualifications for Elders

> "An elder must be blameless, faithful to his wife, a man whose children believe and are not open to the charge of being wild and disobedient."

Elders must be blameless.

Faithful to his wife. His children believe and are well-behaved. Character begins at home.

If a man cannot lead his family, he cannot lead the church.

**Elders must demonstrate godliness at home.**

## Not Overbearing, Not Quick-Tempered

> "Since an overseer manages God's household, he must be blameless—not overbearing, not quick-tempered, not given to drunkenness, not violent, not pursuing dishonest gain."

Elders manage God's household.

They must not be overbearing, quick-tempered, given to drunkenness, violent, or greedy. These disqualify a man from leadership.

**Character disqualifications are serious.**

## Hospitable, Loving What Is Good

> "Rather, he must be hospitable, one who loves what is good, who is self-controlled, upright, holy and disciplined."

Positive qualifications follow.

Hospitable. Loving what is good. Self-controlled, upright, holy, disciplined. These are the marks of spiritual maturity.

**Elders must embody godly character.**

## Holding Firmly to the Trustworthy Message

> "He must hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it has been taught, so that he can encourage others by sound doctrine and refute those who oppose it."

Doctrinal integrity is essential.

Elders must hold firmly to the trustworthy message. They must encourage with sound doctrine and refute false teaching.

**Elders must be doctrinally sound and able to defend the truth.**

## For There Are Many Rebellious People

> "For there are many rebellious people, full of meaningless talk and deception, especially those of the circumcision group."

False teachers abound.

They are rebellious, full of empty talk and deception. The circumcision group insists on law-keeping for salvation.

**False teachers must be silenced.**

## They Must Be Silenced

> "They must be silenced, because they are disrupting whole households by teaching things they ought not to teach—and that for the sake of dishonest gain."

False teaching is destructive.

It disrupts households. It spreads error. And it is often motivated by greed.

Elders must silence such teachers, not through violence but through biblical refutation.

**False teachers disrupt households and must be stopped.**

## Even One of Their Own Prophets Has Said

> "One of Crete's own prophets has said it: 'Cretans are always liars, evil brutes, lazy gluttons.' This saying is true."

Paul quotes a Cretan poet to describe the culture.

The Cretans had a reputation for lying, brutality, and laziness. Paul does not deny this cultural reality.

**Culture shapes character, but the gospel transforms it.**

## Therefore Rebuke Them Sharply

> "Therefore rebuke them sharply, so that they will be sound in the faith and will pay no attention to Jewish myths or to the merely human commands of those who reject the truth."

Sharp rebuke is sometimes necessary.

Not all correction is gentle. Some situations require confrontation. The goal is soundness in the faith.

**Rebuke sharply when necessary to preserve sound doctrine.**

## To the Pure, All Things Are Pure

> "To the pure, all things are pure, but to those who are corrupted and do not believe, nothing is pure. In fact, both their minds and consciences are corrupted."

Purity is internal, not external.

To the pure, all things are pure. External regulations cannot purify the heart.

But to the corrupt, nothing is pure. Their minds and consciences are defiled.

**Purity is a matter of the heart, not external rules.**

## They Claim to Know God, But by Their Actions They Deny Him

> "They claim to know God, but by their actions they deny him. They are detestable, disobedient and unfit for doing anything good."

Profession without practice is hypocrisy.

False teachers claim to know God but deny Him by their actions. Their lives contradict their words.

**True knowledge of God produces obedience.**

## Final Exhortation

Titus 1 establishes the foundation for church health: godly leadership and sound doctrine.

Appoint elders in every town. They must be blameless, managing their households well, holding firmly to the trustworthy message.

False teachers must be silenced. They disrupt households with lies and greed. Rebuke them sharply.

Purity is internal. True knowledge of God produces godly living. Profession without practice is hypocrisy.

Establish order. Appoint qualified leaders. Guard sound doctrine.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, raise up godly elders who hold firmly to Your truth and refute false teaching. Protect Your church from those who disrupt and deceive. Make us pure in heart and obedient in life. In Jesus' name, amen.
