# Sound Doctrine: Live What You Teach

Sound doctrine must produce sound living.

Titus 2 moves from leadership qualifications to practical instruction for every age and social group.

## Teach What Accords With Sound Doctrine

> "You, however, must teach what is appropriate to sound doctrine."

Titus must teach sound doctrine.

Not novelties. Not speculation. Not human wisdom. Only what accords with the apostolic message.

**Teaching must align with sound doctrine.**

## Older Men

> "Teach the older men to be temperate, worthy of respect, self-controlled, and sound in faith, in love and in endurance."

Older men must be temperate, respectable, self-controlled.

Sound in faith, love, and endurance. Age should bring maturity, not stagnation or bitterness.

**Older men should exemplify maturity in faith.**

## Older Women

> "Likewise, teach the older women to be reverent in the way they live, not to be slanderers or addicted to much wine, but to teach what is good."

Older women must be reverent.

Not slanderers or drunkards. They should teach what is good, particularly to younger women.

**Older women should model godliness and mentor others.**

## Train the Younger Women

> "Then they can urge the younger women to love their husbands and children, to be self-controlled and pure, to be busy at home, to be kind, and to be subject to their husbands, so that no one will malign the word of God."

Older women train younger women.

To love husbands and children. To be self-controlled, pure, kind. To manage their households.

The goal is that the Word of God not be blasphemed. Behavior reflects on the gospel.

**Godly living protects the reputation of the gospel.**

## Similarly, Encourage the Young Men

> "Similarly, encourage the young men to be self-controlled. In everything set them an example by doing what is good."

Young men must be self-controlled.

Titus must set an example. Leadership is not just instruction but imitation. Live what you teach.

**Leaders must model what they teach.**

## In Your Teaching Show Integrity

> "In your teaching show integrity, seriousness and soundness of speech that cannot be condemned, so that those who oppose you may be ashamed because they have nothing bad to say about us."

Teaching must demonstrate integrity.

Serious, sound speech that cannot be condemned. This silences opponents. They have nothing to criticize.

**Integrity in teaching silences criticism.**

## Teach Slaves to Be Subject to Their Masters

> "Teach slaves to be subject to their masters in everything, to try to please them, not to talk back to them, and not to steal from them, but to show that they can be fully trusted, so that in every way they will make the teaching about God our Savior attractive."

Even slaves can adorn the gospel.

By being submissive, trustworthy, and diligent, they make the gospel attractive. Their conduct testifies to the transforming power of Christ.

**Every station in life can adorn the gospel.**

## The Grace of God Has Appeared

> "For the grace of God has appeared that offers salvation to all people."

The gospel is for all people.

Grace has appeared in Jesus Christ. Not limited to one nation or one class. Salvation is offered to all.

**Grace has appeared, bringing salvation to all.**

## Teaching Us to Say No to Ungodliness

> "It teaches us to say 'No' to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age."

Grace is not license.

It teaches us to renounce ungodliness and worldly passions. To live self-controlled, upright, and godly lives.

Grace transforms. It does not excuse sin.

**Grace teaches us to say no to sin.**

## While We Wait for the Blessed Hope

> "While we wait for the blessed hope—the appearing of the glory of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ."

The Christian life is lived in expectation.

We wait for the blessed hope: the glorious appearing of Jesus Christ, our great God and Savior.

This hope shapes how we live now.

**We live in light of Christ's return.**

## Who Gave Himself for Us

> "Who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good."

Christ gave Himself to redeem us.

From all wickedness. To purify a people for Himself. A people eager to do good.

Redemption produces transformation. We are saved from sin and for good works.

**Christ redeemed us to make us eager for good works.**

## These, Then, Are the Things You Should Teach

> "These, then, are the things you should teach. Encourage and rebuke with all authority. Do not let anyone despise you."

Titus must teach, encourage, and rebuke.

With authority. Not timidly. Not apologetically. Speak the truth boldly.

**Teach, encourage, and rebuke with authority.**

## Final Exhortation

Titus 2 applies sound doctrine to every group in the church.

Older men: be temperate and sound in faith. Older women: be reverent and teach younger women. Young men: be self-controlled. Slaves: adorn the gospel through faithful service.

Grace has appeared, bringing salvation to all. It teaches us to renounce ungodliness and live godly lives while we wait for Christ's return.

Christ gave Himself to redeem us from wickedness and make us eager for good works.

Teach these things. Encourage and rebuke with authority.

Sound doctrine produces sound living.

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for the grace that has appeared in Christ, teaching us to say no to sin and live godly lives. Help us to adorn the gospel in every area of life as we wait for the blessed hope of Christ's return. In Jesus' name, amen.
