# Sound Doctrine: Saved by Mercy, Not by Works

Titus 3 concludes with gospel theology and practical instructions.

Christian conduct flows from understanding what God has done in Christ.

## Remind the People

> "Remind the people to be subject to rulers and authorities, to be obedient, to be ready to do whatever is good, to slander no one, to be peaceable and considerate, and always to be gentle toward everyone."

Believers must be good citizens.

Subject to authorities. Obedient. Ready for good works. Not slanderers. Peaceable, considerate, gentle.

This is Christian citizenship.

**Believers should be model citizens.**

## At One Time We Too Were Foolish

> "At one time we too were foolish, disobedient, deceived and enslaved by all kinds of passions and pleasures. We lived in malice and envy, being hated and hating one another."

Paul reminds them of their former state.

Foolish, disobedient, deceived, enslaved to passions. Living in malice and envy.

This was us. Do not forget where you came from.

**Remember your former life to cultivate humility.**

## But When the Kindness of God Appeared

> "But when the kindness and love of God our Savior appeared, he saved us, not because of righteous things we had done, but because of his mercy."

The turning point: the appearing of God's kindness.

He saved us. Not because of our righteousness. Because of His mercy.

Salvation is entirely God's work, rooted in His mercy and love.

**We are saved by God's mercy, not our works.**

## He Saved Us Through the Washing of Rebirth

> "He saved us through the washing of rebirth and renewal by the Holy Spirit, whom he poured out on us generously through Jesus Christ our Savior."

Salvation comes through washing and renewal.

The washing of rebirth. The renewal by the Holy Spirit. God poured out the Spirit generously through Jesus Christ.

This is regeneration: God making us new by His Spirit.

**Salvation is regeneration by the Holy Spirit.**

## So That, Having Been Justified by His Grace

> "So that, having been justified by his grace, we might become heirs having the hope of eternal life."

Justification is by grace.

We are declared righteous, not because we earned it, but because God graciously grants it through Christ.

As justified ones, we are heirs. We have the hope of eternal life.

**Justification by grace makes us heirs of eternal life.**

## This Is a Trustworthy Saying

> "This is a trustworthy saying. And I want you to stress these things, so that those who have trusted in God may be careful to devote themselves to doing what is good."

The gospel is trustworthy.

Stress it. Emphasize it. Teach it clearly.

The result: those who trust God will devote themselves to good works.

**Gospel truth produces good works.**

## These Things Are Excellent and Profitable

> "These things are excellent and profitable for everyone."

Good works are excellent and profitable.

Not for salvation, but as the fruit of salvation. They benefit the doer and the community.

**Good works are profitable for all.**

## Avoid Foolish Controversies

> "But avoid foolish controversies and genealogies and arguments and quarrels about the law, because these are unprofitable and useless."

Not all debates are worth having.

Foolish controversies, genealogies, arguments about the law: these are unprofitable and useless.

Focus on what matters. Do not get distracted by trivialities.

**Avoid foolish controversies; they are unprofitable.**

## Warn a Divisive Person

> "Warn a divisive person once, and then warn them a second time. After that, have nothing to do with them."

Divisiveness must be confronted.

Warn once. Warn twice. If they persist, have nothing to do with them.

Unity is precious. Do not tolerate those who sow division.

**Divisive people must be warned and, if necessary, removed.**

## You May Be Sure That Such People Are Warped

> "You may be sure that such people are warped and sinful; they are self-condemned."

Persistent divisiveness reveals a warped heart.

They are sinful and self-condemned. Their actions testify against them.

**Divisiveness is evidence of a warped heart.**

## Final Instructions

> "As soon as I send Artemas or Tychicus to you, do your best to come to me at Nicopolis, because I have decided to winter there."

Paul gives personal instructions.

He plans to send Artemas or Tychicus. Titus should come to Nicopolis.

Ministry is personal and practical.

**Ministry involves personal relationships and practical plans.**

## Do Everything You Can to Help Zenas and Apollos

> "Do everything you can to help Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their way and see that they have everything they need."

Support gospel workers.

Zenas and Apollos need help. Provide for them. Ministry requires logistical and financial support.

**Support those who labor in gospel ministry.**

## Our People Must Learn to Devote Themselves to Doing What Is Good

> "Our people must learn to devote themselves to doing what is good, in order to provide for urgent needs and not live unproductive lives."

Believers must devote themselves to good works.

To meet urgent needs. To avoid unproductive lives. Good works are the evidence of genuine faith.

**Devote yourself to good works to meet needs.**

## Everyone With Me Sends You Greetings

> "Everyone with me sends you greetings. Greet those who love us in the faith. Grace be with you all."

Paul closes with greetings and grace.

Ministry is communal. Greet those who love us in the faith. Grace be with you all.

**Grace is the beginning and end of all ministry.**

## Final Exhortation

Titus 3 grounds Christian conduct in gospel theology.

We were once foolish, disobedient, and enslaved to sin. But God saved us, not because of our works, but because of His mercy.

He saved us through the washing of rebirth and renewal by the Holy Spirit. We are justified by grace and made heirs of eternal life.

This is a trustworthy saying. Stress it. Let it produce devotion to good works.

Avoid foolish controversies. Warn divisive people. Support gospel workers. Devote yourself to good works.

Grace be with you all.

---

## Closing Prayer

Father, thank You for saving us by Your mercy, not by our works. You washed and renewed us by the Holy Spirit and justified us by grace. Help us to devote ourselves to good works, avoid foolish controversies, and live peaceable lives. Grace be with us all. In Jesus' name, amen.
